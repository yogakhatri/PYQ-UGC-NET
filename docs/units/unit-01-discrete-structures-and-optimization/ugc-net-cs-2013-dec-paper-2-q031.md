# Question 31

*UGC NET CS · 2013 Dec Paper 2 · Boolean Algebra · Boolean Functions and their Representation*

The dual of a Boolean expression is obtained by interchanging

- **A.** Boolean sums and Boolean products
- **B.** Boolean sums and Boolean products or interchanging 0’s and 1’s
- **C.** Boolean sums and Boolean products and interchanging 0’s & 1’s
- **D.** Interchanging 0’s and 1’s

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: C. Boolean sums and Boolean products and interchanging 0’s & 1’s**

## Solution

The duality principle for Boolean algebra replaces every OR (+) by AND (·), every AND by OR, every 0 by 1 and every 1 by 0, while variables and complements remain unchanged. Both kinds of interchange must be performed together to obtain the dual expression.

## Key Points

- Boolean dual: swap + with · and 0 with 1 everywhere; do not complement the variables merely to form the dual.

## Why the other options are incorrect

A changes the operations but omits the constants. D changes the constants but omits the operations. B says either transformation is enough, whereas the dual requires both simultaneously.
