# Question 28

*UGC NET CS · 2021 Nov With Answers · Boolean Algebra · Identity, Domination, Idempotent and Absorption Laws*

Match each Boolean identity with its name. A. x+x=x. B. x+0=x. C. x+1=1. D. x+xy=x. I. Identity law. II. Absorption law. III. Idempotent law. IV. Domination law.

- **1.** A ‐ III, B ‐I , C ‐ II, D ‐ IV
- **2.** A ‐ I, B ‐III , C ‐ IV, D ‐ II
- **3.** A ‐ III, B ‐I , C ‐ IV, D ‐ II
- **4.** A ‐ III, B ‐IV , C ‐ I, D ‐ II

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: 3. A ‐ III, B ‐I , C ‐ IV, D ‐ II**

## Solution

In Boolean algebra, x+x=x is the idempotent law, so A→III. The equation x+0=x is the identity law, so B→I. The equation x+1=1 is domination, so C→IV. Finally x+xy=x is absorption, so D→II. The mapping A-III, B-I, C-IV, D-II is option 3.

## Key Points

- 0 is the OR identity, 1 dominates OR, repeated x is idempotent, and x absorbs x·y.

## Why the other options are incorrect

The other options interchange laws with visibly different roles: identity leaves x unchanged using 0, domination forces 1, idempotence removes duplication, and absorption removes the redundant xy term.
