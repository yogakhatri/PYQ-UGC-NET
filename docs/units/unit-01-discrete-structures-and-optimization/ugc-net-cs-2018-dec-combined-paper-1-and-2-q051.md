# Question 51

*UGC NET CS · 2018 Dec Paper 1 And 2 · Mathematical Logic · Statements and Propositions*

In mathematical logic, which of the following are statements ? (i) There will be snow in January. (ii) What is the time now? (iii) Today is Sunday. (iv) You must study Discrete Mathematics. Choose the correct answer from the code given below :

- **1.** (i) and (iii)
- **2.** (i) and (ii)
- **3.** (ii) and (iv)
- **4.** (iii) and (iv)

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: 1. (i) and (iii)**

## Solution

A proposition is a declarative sentence to which exactly one truth value—true or false—can be assigned. Statements (i) and (ii) make definite claims, so each is a proposition even if we have not yet checked whether the claim is true. A question and a command do not assert facts and therefore have no truth value. Hence only (i) and (ii) are propositions, which is option 1.

## Key Points

- Proposition = declarative, unambiguous, truth-valued sentence; questions and commands are not propositions.

## Why the other options are incorrect

Any option containing the interrogative or imperative sentence is incorrect because asking or ordering something does not assert a truth-valued claim. Do not confuse the truth of a proposition with its status as a proposition: a false declarative sentence is still a proposition.
