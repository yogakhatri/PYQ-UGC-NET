# Question 58

*UGC NET CS · 2018 Dec Paper 1 And 2 · Optimization · PERT-CPM Merge Events*

In PERT/CPM, a merge event represents ________ of two or more events.

- **1.** completion
- **2.** beginning
- **3.** splitting
- **4.** joining of two or more events.

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: 4. joining of two or more events.**

## Solution

In an activity-on-arrow PERT/CPM network, a merge event is a node at which two or more preceding activities converge. The succeeding activity cannot begin until every activity entering that event has been completed. This description is called a joining or merging event, so option 4 is correct.

## Key Points

- Multiple arrows entering an event form a merge/join; multiple arrows leaving an event form a burst.

## Why the other options are incorrect

A burst event has several activities leaving one node. A dangling event/branch does not correctly connect to the project completion logic. A redundant activity is unnecessary to the precedence representation; none describes multiple incoming activities.
