# Question 10

*UGC NET CS · 2015 Dec Paper 2 · Group Theory · Abelian Groups*

Which of the following properties must a group G satisfy in order to be Abelian? (a) Distributive property, (b) commutative property, (c) symmetric property.

- **1.** (a) and (b)
- **2.** (b) and (c)
- **3.** (a) only
- **4.** (b) only

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: 4. (b) only**

## Solution

A group is Abelian precisely when its binary operation is commutative: a*b=b*a for all a,b in G. The ordinary group axioms—closure, associativity, identity, and inverses—are already assumed because G is a group. Therefore only property (b), commutativity, is additionally required.

## Key Points

- Abelian group = group + commutative operation.

## Why the other options are incorrect

Distributivity relates two operations and is not a group axiom. ‘Symmetric property’ is not the defining extra condition for an Abelian group. Options 1–3 therefore add an irrelevant property or omit commutativity.
