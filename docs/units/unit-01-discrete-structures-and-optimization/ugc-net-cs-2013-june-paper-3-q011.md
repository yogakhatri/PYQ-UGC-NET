# Question 11

*UGC NET CS · 2013 June Paper 3 · Mathematical Foundations · Golden Ratio and Its Minimal Polynomial*

The golden ratio φ and its algebraic conjugate 1-φ both satisfy which equation?

- **A.** x^3 - x - 1 = 0
- **B.** x^3 + x - 1 = 0
- **C.** x^2 - x - 1 = 0
- **D.** x^2 + x - 1 = 0

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: C. x^2 - x - 1 = 0**

## Solution

The golden ratio is phi=(1+sqrt(5))/2 and its algebraic conjugate is (1-sqrt(5))/2=1-phi. The quadratic formula shows that these are precisely the two roots of x^2-x-1=0. Equivalently, phi^2=phi+1, which rearranges to that polynomial.

## Key Points

- Golden-ratio identity phi^2=phi+1 immediately gives phi^2-phi-1=0.

## Why the other options are incorrect

The two cubic equations have different roots and are not the minimal polynomial of phi. x^2+x-1=0 has roots (-1±sqrt(5))/2, so it includes phi-1 rather than phi itself.
