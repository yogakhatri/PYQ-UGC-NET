# Question 93

*UGC NET CS · 2023 Mar 15 Shift 1 Dec 2022 Session · Sets and Relations · Representation and Properties of Relations*

No.4 BID:8704 The relation R=((1,3), (1,1), (3, 1), (1,2), (3,3), (4,4)} defined in (1,2,3,4} is __

- **1.** Reflexive, Symmetric but not Transitive
- **2.** Symmetric, anti-symmetric and transitive
- **3.** Asymmetric, anti-symmetric and not transitive
- **4.** Not reflexive, not symmetric and not transitive

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: 4. Not reflexive, not symmetric and not transitive**

## Solution

R lacks (2,2), so it is not reflexive. It contains (1,2) without (2,1), so it is not symmetric. Since (3,1) and (1,2) are present but (3,2) is absent, transitivity fails.

## Key Points

- Disprove a relation property with one missing required pair.

## Why the other options are incorrect

Options 1–3 claim at least one property contradicted by these explicit counterexamples.
