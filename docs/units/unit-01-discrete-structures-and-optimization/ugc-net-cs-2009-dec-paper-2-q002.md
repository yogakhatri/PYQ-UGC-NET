# Question 2

*UGC NET CS · 2009 Dec Paper 2 · Optimization · Cost Consideration in Project Scheduling*

Circle has ____________

- **A.** No vertices
- **B.** Only 1 vertex
- **C.** ∞ vertices
- **D.** None of these

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: A. No vertices**

## Solution

A vertex is a distinguished corner or meeting point of edges. A geometric circle is a smooth closed curve with no corners or endpoints, so it has no vertices.

## Key Points

- Points on a smooth curve are not vertices unless a graph structure explicitly declares them so.

## Why the other options are incorrect

A circle does not have one distinguished corner, and its infinitely many points are not all called vertices in ordinary geometry.
