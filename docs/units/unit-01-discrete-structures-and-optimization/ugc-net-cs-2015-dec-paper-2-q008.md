# Question 8

*UGC NET CS · 2015 Dec Paper 2 · Proof Techniques · Vacuous, Trivial, Direct and Indirect Proofs*

Match the proof methods with their descriptions: (a) vacuous proof—(i) prove p → q because p is false; (b) trivial proof—(ii) prove p → q because q is true; (c) direct proof—(iii) assume p and show q; (d) indirect proof—(iv) show that p must be false when q is false.

- **1.** (i) (ii) (iii) (iv)
- **2.** (ii) (iii) (i) (iv)
- **3.** (iii) (ii) (iv) (i)
- **4.** (iv) (iii) (ii) (i)

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: 1. (i) (ii) (iii) (iv)**

## Solution

A vacuous proof establishes p→q because the antecedent p is false, so (a)–(i). A trivial proof establishes it because q is already true, so (b)–(ii). A direct proof assumes p and derives q, so (c)–(iii). An indirect proof here proves the contrapositive ¬q→¬p, so (d)–(iv). The sequence is (i),(ii),(iii),(iv).

## Key Points

- Vacuous: false antecedent; trivial: true consequent; direct: p to q; indirect: ¬q to ¬p.

## Why the other options are incorrect

The other codes interchange the defining roles of a false antecedent, a true consequent, direct implication, and contrapositive reasoning.
