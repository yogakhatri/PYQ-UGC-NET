# Question 2

*UGC NET CS · 2017 Jan Paper 2 · Mathematical Logic · Axioms, Conjectures, and Mathematical Terminology*

Match the following : List – I List – II a. Absurd i. Clearly impossible being contrary to some evident truth. b. Ambiguous ii. Capable of more than one interpretation or meaning. c. Axiom iii. An assertion that is accepted and used without a proof. d. Conjecture iv. An opinion preferably based on some experience or wisdom. Codes : a b c d

- **1.** i ii iii iv
- **2.** i iii iv ii
- **3.** ii iii iv i
- **4.** ii i iii iv

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: 1. i ii iii iv**

## Solution

Absurd means clearly impossible or contrary to evident truth, so a→i. Ambiguous means capable of multiple interpretations, so b→ii. An axiom is accepted as a starting assertion without proof, so c→iii. A conjecture is a proposed opinion or claim supported by observation or experience but not yet proved, so d→iv. The sequence i, ii, iii, iv is option 1.

## Key Points

- Axiom = accepted starting point; conjecture = plausible unproved claim; ambiguous = multiple meanings; absurd = evidently impossible.

## Why the other options are incorrect

The remaining codes interchange terms with definitions that have sharply different logical status: an axiom is assumed, a conjecture awaits proof, ambiguity concerns multiple meanings, and absurdity concerns contradiction or impossibility.
