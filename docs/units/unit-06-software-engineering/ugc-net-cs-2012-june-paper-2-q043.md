# Question 43

*UGC NET CS · 2012 June Paper 2 · Software Reliability · Residual Faults and Reliability*

Reliability of software is directly dependent on

- **A.** Quality of the design
- **B.** Number of errors present
- **C.** Software engineer's experience
- **D.** User requirement

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: B. Number of errors present**

## Solution

Software reliability is the probability of failure-free operation for a specified time and environment. Failures arise when residual faults/errors are activated by inputs and operational conditions. Consequently reliability is most directly related to how many defects remain and how likely the operational profile is to encounter them; more residual errors generally mean lower reliability.

## Key Points

- Reliability falls as residual fault content and fault exposure rise; design and experience affect reliability through that fault content.

## Why the other options are incorrect

Design quality and engineer experience influence how many faults are introduced or removed, but they are indirect factors. User requirements define expected behaviour and acceptance but do not themselves determine the realized failure rate.
