# Question 17

*UGC NET CS · 2021 Nov With Answers · Software Process Models · Waterfall, Evolutionary, Component-Based and Spiral Models*

Match each software process model with its description. A. Waterfall model. B. Evolutionary model. C. Component-based software engineering. D. Spiral development. I. Software can be developed incrementally. II. Requirement compromises are inevitable. III. Explicit recognition of risk. IV. Inflexible partitioning of the project into stages.

- **1.** A ‐ IV, B ‐ I, C ‐ III, D ‐ II
- **2.** A ‐ I, B ‐ IV, C ‐ II, D ‐ III
- **3.** A ‐ II, B ‐ III, C ‐ I, D ‐ IV
- **4.** A ‐ IV, B ‐ I, C ‐ II, D ‐ III

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: 4. A ‐ IV, B ‐ I, C ‐ II, D ‐ III**

## Solution

Waterfall fixes a sequence of distinct stages and is difficult to revisit, so A→IV. Evolutionary development delivers and refines the product in increments, so B→I. Component-based engineering must adapt requirements to the capabilities and interfaces of available components, making compromises inevitable, so C→II. Spiral development makes risk identification and reduction central to every cycle, so D→III. The complete mapping is option 4.

## Key Points

- Waterfall=rigid stages; evolutionary=increments; components=require compromise; spiral=risk-driven cycles.

## Why the other options are incorrect

The other mappings assign explicit risk management away from the spiral model or confuse evolutionary increments with component constraints. Those are the distinguishing features of the four models.
