# Question 31

*UGC NET CS · 2015 Dec Paper 3 · Software Design and Review · CRC Review-Team Composition*

In CRC-based design, a CRC team consists of: (a) one or two user representatives, (b) several programmers, (c) project coordinators, and (d) one or two system analysts. Which members are included?

- **1.** (a) and (c)
- **2.** (a), (b), (c) and (d)
- **3.** (a), (c) and (d)
- **4.** (a), (b) and (d)

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: 3. (a), (c) and (d)**

## Solution

In the CRC/critical-review-team model intended here, the review group represents stakeholders and project oversight: one or two user representatives, project coordinators, and one or two system analysts. Several programmers are not part of the prescribed core team. Therefore the included members are (a), (c), and (d).

## Key Points

- A review team balances user, coordination, and systems-analysis perspectives rather than being a programming team.

## Why the other options are incorrect

Option 1 omits the system analysts. Options 2 and 4 include several programmers, which the stated review-team composition excludes.
