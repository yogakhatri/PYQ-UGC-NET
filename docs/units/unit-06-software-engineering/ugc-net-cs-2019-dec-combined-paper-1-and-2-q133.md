# Question 133

*UGC NET CS · 2019 Dec Paper 1 And 2 · Software Quality · McCall and ISO 9126 Factors*

According to the ISO 9126 quality model, match List I with List II. List I: A. Functionality; B. Reliability; C. Efficiency; D. Maintainability. List II: I. relationship between the level of performance and the amount of resources used; II. characteristics related to achieving the stated purpose; III. effort needed to make an improvement; IV. capability of software to maintain its level of performance.

- **1.** A-I, B-II, C-III, D-IV
- **2.** A-II, B-I, C-IV, D-III
- **3.** A-II, B-IV, C-I, D-III
- **4.** A-I, B-II, C-IV, D-III

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: 3. A-II, B-IV, C-I, D-III**

## Solution

In ISO 9126, functionality concerns whether the software provides functions that meet stated needs—achievement of purpose—so A-II. Reliability is the capability to maintain a specified level of performance under stated conditions, so B-IV. Efficiency relates performance to the resources used, so C-I. Maintainability concerns the effort needed to analyse, modify, or improve the software, so D-III. This gives A-II, B-IV, C-I, D-III, which is option 3.

## Key Points

- ISO 9126: functionality meets needs, reliability sustains performance, efficiency balances performance and resources, maintainability measures modification effort.

## Why the other options are incorrect

Option 1 assigns the resource-performance relationship to functionality and improvement effort to efficiency. Option 2 swaps reliability and efficiency. Option 4 gives both functionality and reliability the wrong definitions. The noun in each definition—purpose, maintained performance, resources, improvement—points directly to the factor.
