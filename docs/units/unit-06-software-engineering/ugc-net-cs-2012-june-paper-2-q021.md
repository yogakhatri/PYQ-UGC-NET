# Question 21

*UGC NET CS · 2012 June Paper 2 · Software Quality and Process Improvement · CMM Maturity Levels*

The key process areas of CMM Level 4 are also satisfied by a process at which level?

- **A.** CMM level 2
- **B.** CMM level 3
- **C.** CMM level 5
- **D.** All of the above

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: C. CMM level 5**

## Solution

CMM maturity levels are cumulative. An organization assessed at Level 5 must institutionalize the lower-level practices and also add Level 5's optimizing practices. It therefore satisfies the quantitative process-management and software quality-management key process areas associated with Level 4. A Level 2 or Level 3 process has not yet demonstrated Level 4 control.

## Key Points

- CMM levels are staged and cumulative: a Level 5 process includes the capabilities required at Levels 2-4.

## Why the other options are incorrect

Levels 2 and 3 are below Level 4 and need not satisfy its quantitative KPAs, so A and B are false. Because those lower levels are not all correct, 'all of the above' is false.
