# Question 9

*UGC NET CS · 2013 Dec Paper 2 · Requirements Engineering · Requirements-Engineering Activities*

Which one of the following is not a step of requirement engineering ?

- **A.** Requirement elicitation
- **B.** Requirement analysis
- **C.** Requirement design
- **D.** Requirement documentation

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: C. Requirement design**

## Solution

Requirements engineering includes eliciting stakeholder needs, analyzing and negotiating them, documenting/specifying the agreed requirements, validating them and managing changes. 'Requirement design' is not a standard requirements-engineering step; design begins deciding how the system will satisfy requirements.

## Key Points

- Requirements state what is needed; design decides how to build it.
- Do not mix the design phase into requirements engineering.

## Why the other options are incorrect

Elicitation discovers needs, analysis resolves and models them, and documentation records them in an agreed form. All three are recognized requirements activities.
