# Question 43

*UGC NET CS · 2017 Nov Paper 2 · Software Development Methods and Tools · Agile Philosophy and Continuous Testing*

Which of the following is not a key issue stressed by an agile philosophy of software engineering ?

- **1.** The importance of self-organizing teams as well as communication and collaboration between team members and customers.
- **2.** Recognition that change represents opportunity.
- **3.** Emphasis on rapid delivery of software that satisfies the customer.
- **4.** Having a separate testing phase after a build phase.

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: 4. Having a separate testing phase after a build phase.**

## Solution

Agile development favors small increments in which building, testing, feedback, and adaptation happen continuously. A separate testing phase only after a build phase reflects a staged waterfall-style handoff, not a key agile principle. Therefore option 4 is the requested answer.

## Key Points

- Agile shortens feedback loops by integrating development and testing in every increment.

## Why the other options are incorrect

Self-organizing collaborative teams, welcoming change, and rapid delivery of customer-valued software are central agile themes. Testing is still essential in agile, but it is integrated into iterations and often automated rather than postponed as a separate late phase.
