# Question 23

*UGC NET CS · 2012 June Paper 2 · Software Quality and Process Improvement · Statistical Process Control*

If a process is under statistical control, then it is

- **A.** Maintainable
- **B.** Measurable
- **C.** Predictable
- **D.** Verifiable

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: C. Predictable**

## Solution

A statistically controlled process varies only within established control limits because special causes have been removed or understood. Its future performance is therefore stable enough to predict within a statistical range. Predictability is what makes quantitative process management useful.

## Key Points

- Statistical control means stable variation, which makes process outcomes predictable within limits.

## Why the other options are incorrect

Maintainability is a product/process-change quality, measurability is a prerequisite for statistical analysis, and verifiability concerns checking results. None is the direct consequence expressed by statistical control.
