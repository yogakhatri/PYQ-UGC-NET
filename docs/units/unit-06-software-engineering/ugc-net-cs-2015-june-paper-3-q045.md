# Question 45

*UGC NET CS · 2015 June Paper 3 · Software Requirements · Prioritization and Negotiation during Elicitation*

Requirements prioritisation and negotiation belongs to :

- **1.** Requirements validation
- **2.** Requirements elicitation
- **3.** Feasibility study
- **4.** Requirements reviews

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: 2. Requirements elicitation**

## Solution

Requirements elicitation and analysis is iterative: stakeholders propose needs, analysts classify them, conflicts are exposed, and the parties prioritize and negotiate a feasible agreed set. Among the supplied process stages, prioritization and negotiation therefore belong with requirements elicitation.

## Key Points

- Elicitation is not only collection—it includes reconciling conflicts and agreeing priorities with stakeholders.

## Why the other options are incorrect

Validation checks that documented requirements are correct, complete, consistent, and testable; reviews are one validation technique. A feasibility study evaluates whether the project is viable before detailed agreement. Neither is the main home of stakeholder priority negotiation in this textbook process model.
