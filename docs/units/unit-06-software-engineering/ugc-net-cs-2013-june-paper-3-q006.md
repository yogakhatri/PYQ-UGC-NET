# Question 6

*UGC NET CS · 2013 June Paper 3 · Software Quality · Software Safety and Hazard Analysis*

Software safety is a __________ activity that focuses on the identification and assessment of potential hazards that may affect software negatively and cause an entire system to fail.

- **A.** Risk mitigation, monitoring and management
- **B.** Software quality assurance
- **C.** Software cost estimation
- **D.** Defect removal efficiency

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: B. Software quality assurance**

## Solution

Software safety is treated as a software quality-assurance activity because it identifies hazards, estimates their severity/probability and imposes requirements or controls that prevent software behavior from causing system-level harm. Safety analysis is therefore part of assuring the product's quality and dependability throughout development.

## Key Points

- Software safety extends SQA from ordinary defects to software-induced hazards and system failure.

## Why the other options are incorrect

Risk mitigation, monitoring and management is a broader project-risk framework; safety can feed that framework but the quoted definition classifies it as SQA. Cost estimation predicts effort/budget. Defect-removal efficiency is a metric comparing defects removed before release with total defects, not hazard analysis.
