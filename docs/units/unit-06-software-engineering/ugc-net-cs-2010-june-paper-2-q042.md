# Question 42

*UGC NET CS · 2010 June Paper 2 · Software Design · UI and Component-Level Design*

Top-down design does not require

- **A.** step-wise refinement
- **B.** loop invariants
- **C.** flow charting
- **D.** modularity

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: B. loop invariants**

## Solution

Top-down design decomposes a system from high-level modules into progressively more detailed submodules. It relies on stepwise refinement and modularity, and may use flowcharts to express control. Loop invariants are a program-verification technique for proving loop correctness, not a requirement of top-down design.

## Key Points

- Do not confuse a design/decomposition method with a formal loop-proof technique.

## Why the other options are incorrect

Stepwise refinement is the essence of top-down decomposition; modularity is its structural result; flowcharting is a compatible design notation.
