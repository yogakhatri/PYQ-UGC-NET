# Question 46

*UGC NET CS · 2017 Jan Paper 3 · Estimation and Scheduling · Function-Point Productivity and Project Cost*

A software project is estimated at 352 Function Points. A four-person team consists of one architect, two programmers, and one tester. Monthly salaries are ₹80,000 for the architect, ₹60,000 per programmer, and ₹50,000 for the tester. Average productivity is 8 FP per person-month. What is the projected project cost?

- **1.** ₹28,16,000
- **2.** ₹20,90,000
- **3.** ₹26,95,000
- **4.** ₹27,50,000

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: 4. ₹27,50,000**

## Solution

At 8 FP per person-month, 352 FP requires 352/8=44 person-months of effort. With four people, the calendar duration is 44/4=11 months. The team's monthly salary is ₹80,000+2(₹60,000)+₹50,000=₹2,50,000. Hence projected labor cost is 11×₹2,50,000=₹27,50,000, which is option 4.

## Key Points

- Person-month effort = FP/productivity; duration = effort/team size; cost = duration×team monthly payroll.

## Why the other options are incorrect

The other amounts do not equal the required person-month effort converted into an 11-month team duration and multiplied by the actual role-weighted monthly payroll. Multiplying 44 directly by the whole team's monthly cost would count four times too much.
