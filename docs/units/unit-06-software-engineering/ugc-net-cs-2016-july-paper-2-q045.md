# Question 45

*UGC NET CS · 2016 July Paper 2 · Software Quality · Robustness versus Reliability and Correctness*

The extent to which a software tolerates the unexpected problems, is termed as :

- **1.** Accuracy
- **2.** Reliability
- **3.** Correctness
- **4.** Robustness

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: 4. Robustness**

## Solution

Robustness is a system's ability to continue behaving acceptably when it encounters invalid input, unusual conditions, environmental faults, or other unexpected problems. The wording therefore directly identifies robustness, option 4.

## Key Points

- Robustness means graceful behavior under unexpected or erroneous conditions; reliability means sustained failure-free operation.

## Why the other options are incorrect

Accuracy concerns closeness to the correct value. Correctness concerns conformity to specified requirements. Reliability concerns the probability of failure-free operation over a stated time and environment. These qualities matter, but tolerance of unexpected conditions is specifically robustness.
