# Question 135

*UGC NET CS · 2020 Nov With Answers · Software Quality · Quality Control and Assurance*

Statement I: Quality control involves the inspections, reviews, and tests used throughout the software process to ensure that each work product meets its requirements. Statement II: Quality assurance consists of auditing and reporting functions of management. Choose the correct evaluation.

- **1.** Both Statement I and Statement II are true
- **2.** Both Statement I and Statement Il are false
- **3.** Statement I is correct but Statement II is false
- **4.** Statement I is incorrect but Statement Il is true.

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: 1. Both Statement I and Statement II are true**

## Solution

Quality control is product-focused: inspections, reviews, and testing detect whether each software work product conforms to its stated requirements. Quality assurance is process- and management-focused: it audits how the process is being followed and reports evidence to management. The two statements describe these complementary roles correctly, so both are true and option 1 is correct.

## Key Points

- QC asks whether the product is correct; QA asks whether the process provides confidence and is being followed.

## Why the other options are incorrect

Options 2–4 make at least one correct definition false. QC does not replace QA: QC examines deliverables, whereas QA supplies confidence and visibility through planned process activities and audits.
