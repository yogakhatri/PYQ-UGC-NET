# Question 7

*UGC NET CS · 2013 Dec Paper 2 · Software Design · Cohesion and Coupling*

The relationship of data elements in a module is called

- **A.** Coupling
- **B.** Modularity
- **C.** Cohesion
- **D.** Granularity

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: C. Cohesion**

## Solution

Cohesion describes how strongly the data elements and operations inside one module belong together in performing a focused purpose. Good design seeks high cohesion so a module has a clear responsibility and changes for one concern remain localized.

## Key Points

- Cohesion is within a module; coupling is between modules.
- Prefer high cohesion and low coupling.

## Why the other options are incorrect

Coupling measures dependence between different modules. Modularity is the overall division of a system into modules, and granularity describes their relative size or level of detail. Neither names the internal relationship asked here.
