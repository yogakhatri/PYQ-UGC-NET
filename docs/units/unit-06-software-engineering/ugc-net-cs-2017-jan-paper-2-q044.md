# Question 44

*UGC NET CS · 2017 Jan Paper 2 · Software Process Models · Prototyping, Iterative and Timeboxed Models*

The prototyping model of software development is :

- **1.** a reasonable approach when requirements are well-defined.
- **2.** a useful approach when a customer cannot define requirements clearly.
- **3.** the best approach to use for projects with large development teams.
- **4.** a risky model that rarely produces a meaningful product.

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: 2. a useful approach when a customer cannot define requirements clearly.**

## Solution

A prototype gives the customer something concrete to examine, making hidden needs, misunderstandings, interface expectations, and priorities easier to discover. It is especially useful when the customer cannot state requirements clearly at the outset. Therefore option 2 is correct.

## Key Points

- Prototype to learn and clarify uncertain requirements through early feedback.

## Why the other options are incorrect

When requirements are already stable and complete, prototyping offers less discovery value. Team size alone does not make it the best process model. Prototypes can be throwaway or evolutionary and often provide valuable learning; they do not 'rarely' produce meaningful outcomes.
