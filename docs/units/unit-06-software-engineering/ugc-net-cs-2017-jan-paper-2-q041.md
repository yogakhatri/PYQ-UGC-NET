# Question 41

*UGC NET CS · 2017 Jan Paper 2 · Software Engineering Foundations · Scope of Software Engineering*

Software Engineering is an engineering discipline that is concerned with :

- **1.** how computer systems work.
- **2.** theories and methods that underlie computers and software systems.
- **3.** all aspects of software production
- **4.** all aspects of computer-based systems development, including hardware, software and process engineering.

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: 3. all aspects of software production**

## Solution

Software engineering applies systematic engineering principles, methods, tools, management, and quality practices throughout requirements, development, deployment, evolution, and maintenance. It is therefore concerned with all aspects of software production, as stated in option 3.

## Key Points

- Computer science studies foundations; software engineering manages and performs the complete production and evolution of software; systems engineering spans the whole computer-based system.

## Why the other options are incorrect

Option 1 focuses on computer operation and architecture. Option 2 describes foundational computer-science theory. Option 4 is the broader discipline of systems engineering because it explicitly includes hardware and overall process engineering, not software engineering alone.
