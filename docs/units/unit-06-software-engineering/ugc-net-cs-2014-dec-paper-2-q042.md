# Question 42

*UGC NET CS · 2014 Dec Paper 2 · Software Process Improvement · CMMI Maturity-Level 3 Process Areas*

Requirements Development, Organizational Process Focus, Organizational Training, Risk Management and Integrated Supplier Management are process areas required to achieve CMMI maturity level

- **A.** Performed
- **B.** Managed
- **C.** Defined
- **D.** Optimized

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: C. Defined**

## Solution

In staged CMMI, maturity level 3 is Defined. Its process areas include Requirements Development, Organizational Process Focus, Organizational Process Definition, Organizational Training, Integrated Project Management, Risk Management, Decision Analysis and Resolution, and—under the relevant model version—Integrated Supplier Management. The listed group therefore identifies the Defined level.

## Key Points

- CMMI levels: 1 Performed, 2 Managed, 3 Defined, 4 Quantitatively Managed, 5 Optimizing.

## Why the other options are incorrect

Performed is level 1 and has no defined set of institutionalized process areas. Managed is level 2, centered on project management basics such as requirements management and measurement. Optimizing is level 5, focused on causal analysis and organizational innovation/performance improvement.
