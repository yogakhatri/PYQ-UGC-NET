# Question 60

*UGC NET CS · 2014 Dec Paper 3 · Information Systems · Organizational Need for Computer-Based Information Systems*

A computer based information system is needed : I. as it is difficult for administrative staff to process data. II. due to rapid growth of information and communication technology. III. due to growing size of organizations which need to process large volume of data. IV. as timely and accurate decisions are to be taken. Which of the above statement(s) is/are true ?

- **A.** I and II
- **B.** III and IV
- **C.** II and III
- **D.** II and IV

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: B. III and IV**

## Solution

A computer-based information system is justified when a growing organization must process a large volume of data (III) and when decisions require timely, accurate information (IV). These are direct information-processing and decision-support needs. Therefore III and IV form the correct pair.

## Key Points

- Justify an information system by the organization's data scale and decision-information requirements, not merely by staff difficulty or technology's existence.

## Why the other options are incorrect

I is an overgeneralization: administrative staff are not inherently unable to process data, although automation can improve scale and efficiency. II describes growth in the enabling technology, not an organizational information need by itself. Every alternative containing I or II therefore misses the intended needs-based distinction.
