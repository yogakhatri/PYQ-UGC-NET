# Question 22

*UGC NET CS · 2012 June Paper 2 · Software Testing · Verification and Validation*

Validation means

- **A.** are we building the product right
- **B.** are we building the right product
- **C.** verification of fields
- **D.** None of the above

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: B. are we building the right product**

## Solution

Validation checks whether the completed or specified system is the product stakeholders actually need: 'Are we building the right product?' It compares behaviour with user needs, intended use and acceptance criteria.

## Key Points

- Verification checks conformance; validation checks fitness for user need.

## Why the other options are incorrect

'Are we building the product right?' is verification—checking conformance to specifications and correct construction. Field verification is not the definition, so C and D are also incorrect.
