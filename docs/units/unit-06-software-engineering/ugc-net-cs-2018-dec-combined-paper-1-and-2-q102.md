# Question 102

*UGC NET CS · 2018 Dec Paper 1 And 2 · Software Requirements · SRS Consistency*

The Software Requirement Specification (SRS) is said to be _____ if and only if no subset of individual requirements described in it conflict with each other.

- **1.** Correct
- **2.** Consistent
- **3.** Unambiguous
- **4.** Verifiable

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: 2. Consistent**

## Solution

An SRS is consistent when its requirements do not contradict one another. The question states precisely that no subset of the individual requirements conflicts with another, which is the consistency property. Therefore option 2 is correct.

## Key Points

- Use the direct definition: consistency = no mutually conflicting requirements in the specification.

## Why the other options are incorrect

Correct means the SRS reflects the customer's actual needs. Unambiguous means every requirement has only one interpretation. Verifiable means an objective test or check can determine whether the requirement is satisfied. None of those terms specifically denotes absence of conflicts among requirements.
