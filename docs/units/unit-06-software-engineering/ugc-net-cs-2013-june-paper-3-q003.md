# Question 3

*UGC NET CS · 2013 June Paper 3 · Software Process Models · Cleanroom Software Engineering*

_____ is a process model that removes defects before they can precipitate serious hazards.

- **A.** Incremental model
- **B.** Spiral model
- **C.** Cleanroom software engineering
- **D.** Agile model

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: C. Cleanroom software engineering**

## Solution

Cleanroom software engineering aims to prevent defects rather than rely on finding and repairing them after execution. It combines formal specification/design, correctness verification and statistically based usage testing. Removing defects before they can trigger serious failures or hazards is therefore the Cleanroom philosophy described by the stem.

## Key Points

- Cleanroom emphasizes correctness by construction and statistical quality certification-defect prevention before failure.

## Why the other options are incorrect

Incremental and Agile models organize development and delivery but do not inherently guarantee defect prevention. The spiral model is risk-driven and repeatedly evaluates risks, yet the specific defect-prevention process named in the question is Cleanroom.
