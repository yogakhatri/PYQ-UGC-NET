# Question 83

*UGC NET CS · 2019 June Paper 1 And 2 · Data Modeling · Set-theoretic relational operations*

With respect to relational algebra, which operations are included from mathematical set theory? (a) Join; (b) Intersection; (c) Cartesian product; (d) Project.

- **1.** (a) and (d)
- **2.** (b) and (c)
- **3.** (c) and (d)
- **4.** (b) and (d)

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: 2. (b) and (c)**

## Solution

Relational algebra borrows union, intersection, difference and Cartesian product directly from mathematical set theory. From the listed operations, intersection and Cartesian product are therefore the set-theoretic pair. Join and projection are relational-algebra operations defined specifically for relations.

## Key Points

- Set-theoretic relational operations include union, intersection, difference and Cartesian product.

## Why the other options are incorrect

Options 1, 3 and 4 include join or projection, so they are not composed solely of operations inherited from set theory.
