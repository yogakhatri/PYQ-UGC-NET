# Question 49

*UGC NET CS · 2014 Dec Paper 2 · E-Business Pricing Models · Demand-Sensitive Pricing*

Which model is designed to bring prices down by increasing the number of customers who buy a particular product at once?

- **A.** Economic Order Quantity
- **B.** Inventory
- **C.** Data Mining
- **D.** Demand-Sensitive Pricing

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: D. Demand-Sensitive Pricing**

## Solution

Demand-sensitive pricing lowers the unit price when aggregate demand or the size of a buying group rises. By encouraging more customers to commit to the same product at once, the model creates volume economies and shares part of the saving through a lower price.

## Key Points

- Demand-sensitive/group-buying pricing makes price fall as committed purchase volume rises.

## Why the other options are incorrect

Economic order quantity optimizes inventory ordering cost and holding cost. Inventory is a stock concept, not a pricing model. Data mining discovers patterns but does not itself define the group-volume price mechanism.
