# Question 13

*UGC NET CS · 2013 Dec Paper 2 · Data Modeling · ER Model Components*

An ER Model includes I. An ER diagram portraying entity types. II. Attributes for each entity type III. Relationships among entity types. IV. Semantic integrity constraints that reflects the business rules about data not captured in the ER diagram.

- **A.** I, II, III & IV
- **B.** I & IV
- **C.** I, II & IV
- **D.** I & III

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: A. I, II, III & IV**

## Solution

An ER model identifies entity types, their attributes and the relationship types connecting them. A complete conceptual data model also records semantic integrity constraints and business rules that the diagram's basic shapes may not fully express. Statements I, II, III and IV therefore all belong to the ER modeling result.

## Key Points

- A conceptual ER model combines entities, attributes, relationships and relevant semantic constraints.

## Why the other options are incorrect

Options B, C and D each omit at least one essential structural element, such as attributes or relationships. Excluding semantic rules would also leave important domain meaning undocumented.
