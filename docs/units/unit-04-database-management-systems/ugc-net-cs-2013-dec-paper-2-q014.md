# Question 14

*UGC NET CS · 2013 Dec Paper 2 · ER-to-Relational Mapping · Cardinality and Participation Constraints*

Based on the cardinality ratio and participation ________ associated with a relationship type, choose either the Foreign Key Design, the Cross Referencing Design or Mutual Referencing Design.

- **A.** Entity
- **B.** Constraints
- **C.** Rules
- **D.** Keys

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: B. Constraints**

## Solution

Cardinality ratio and participation are structural constraints of a relationship type. They determine whether foreign keys can be placed on one side, whether a separate cross-reference relation is needed, and whether mutual references are appropriate when translating an ER model into a database design.

## Key Points

- ER relationship constraints include cardinality (1:1, 1:N, M:N) and participation (total or partial); these guide relational mapping.

## Why the other options are incorrect

Entities and keys are model components affected by the design, but the phrase 'cardinality ratio and participation' specifically names relationship constraints. 'Rules' is too general and is not the formal completion used here.
