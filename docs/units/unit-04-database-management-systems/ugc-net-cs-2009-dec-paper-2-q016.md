# Question 16

*UGC NET CS · 2009 Dec Paper 2 · Data Modeling · Relational Model - Constraints*

The E-R model is expressed in term of I. Entities II. The relationship among entities. III. The attributes of the entities. IV. Functional relationship.

- **A.** I, II
- **B.** I, II, IV
- **C.** II, II, IV
- **D.** I, II, III

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: D. I, II, III**

## Solution

The ER model describes entity sets, their attributes, and relationship sets connecting entities. These are statements I, II, and III. A 'functional relationship' is not a fourth basic modeling component in the same sense.

## Key Points

- ER stands for entities, their properties, and relationships among them.

## Why the other options are incorrect

Options A and B omit attributes or add the nonbasic item IV; option C is malformed and omits entities.
