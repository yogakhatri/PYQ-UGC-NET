# Question 48

*UGC NET CS · 2014 Dec Paper 2 · E-Business Models · Consumer-to-Business Pricing*

Which e-business model allows consumers to name their own price for products and services?

- **A.** B2B
- **B.** B2G
- **C.** C2C
- **D.** C2B

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: D. C2B**

## Solution

In consumer-to-business (C2B), an individual consumer offers value or terms to a business. Reverse-auction and 'name your own price' services are classic examples: consumers state a price and businesses decide whether to accept or compete for the transaction.

## Key Points

- C2B reverses the usual seller-led direction: the consumer proposes an offer to businesses.

## Why the other options are incorrect

B2B is commerce between businesses. B2G involves businesses supplying government. C2C connects consumers with other consumers, such as a peer marketplace; it does not specifically describe businesses accepting consumer-proposed prices.
