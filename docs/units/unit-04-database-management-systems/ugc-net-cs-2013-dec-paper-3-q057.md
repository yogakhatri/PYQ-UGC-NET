# Question 57

*UGC NET CS · 2013 Dec Paper 3 · Entity-Relationship Model · Weak Relationships and Conceptual Models*

Find the false statement :

- **A.** The relationship construct known as the weak relationship type was defined by Dey, Storey & Barron (1999)
- **B.** A weak relationship occurs when two relationship types are linked by either Event- Precedent sequence or Condition-Precedent sequence.
- **C.** Conceptual model is not accurate representation of “Universe of interest”.
- **D.** Ternary, Quaternary and Quintary relationships are shown through a series of application scenario’s and vignette’s.

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: C. Conceptual model is not accurate representation of “Universe of interest”.**

## Solution

A conceptual database model is intended to give an accurate, implementation-independent representation of the relevant universe of interest (also called the universe of discourse), while abstracting away irrelevant detail. Statement C denies that defining purpose and is therefore the false statement. The weak-relationship construct and the treatment of higher-arity relationships in the other statements refer to the relationship-analysis framework described by Dey, Storey and Barron.

## Key Points

- A conceptual model should faithfully represent the relevant concepts and relationships in its universe of discourse, without committing to storage details.

## Why the other options are incorrect

A correctly attributes the weak relationship type to the 1999 Dey-Storey-Barron work. B describes the event-precedent and condition-precedent links used in that framework. D is consistent with its use of scenarios and vignettes to illustrate ternary and higher-arity relationships.
