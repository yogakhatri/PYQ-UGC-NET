# Question 146

*UGC NET CS · 2019 June Paper 1 And 2 · Fuzzy Sets · Defuzzification*

Consider M1: mean of maximum, M2: centre of area, and M3: height method. Which are defuzzification methods?

- **1.** Only M2
- **2.** Only M1 and M2
- **3.** Only M2 and M3
- **4.** M1, M2 and M3

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: 4. M1, M2 and M3**

## Solution

All three convert an output fuzzy set or aggregated rule output into a crisp value. Mean of maximum averages the locations attaining maximum membership. Centre of area computes the centroid of the membership-area distribution. The height method forms a weighted average using rule-output heights. Therefore M1, M2 and M3 are all defuzzification methods.

## Key Points

- Defuzzification maps a fuzzy output to one crisp representative value; centroid, mean-of-maxima and height are standard methods.

## Why the other options are incorrect

Options 1-3 each omit at least one recognized method. They differ in how they summarize the fuzzy output, but all serve the same crisp-output purpose.
