# Question 45

*UGC NET CS · 2015 Dec Paper 3 · Expert Systems · Reasoning Strategies*

Reasoning strategies used in expert systems include __________.

- **1.** Forward chaining, backward chaining and problem reduction
- **2.** Forward chaining, backward chaining and boundary mutation
- **3.** Forward chaining, backward chaining and back propagation
- **4.** Backward chaining, problem reduction and boundary mutation

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: 1. Forward chaining, backward chaining and problem reduction**

## Solution

Expert systems commonly reason by forward chaining from facts, backward chaining from goals, and problem reduction that decomposes a goal into smaller subproblems. These are symbolic inference/search strategies, so option 1 is correct.

## Key Points

- Expert-system inference is rule/search based: forward, backward, and decomposition/problem reduction.

## Why the other options are incorrect

Boundary mutation is not a standard expert-system inference strategy. Backpropagation trains neural networks rather than performing rule-based expert-system reasoning. Options 2–4 therefore include an unrelated technique.
