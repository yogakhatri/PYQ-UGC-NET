# Question 47

*UGC NET CS · 2015 Dec Paper 3 · Constraint Satisfaction · Constraints, Variables and Domains*

In constraint satisfaction problem, constraints can be stated as __________.

- **1.** Arithmetic equations and inequalities that bind the values of variables
- **2.** Arithmetic equations and inequalities that do not bind any restriction over variables
- **3.** Arithmetic equations that impose restrictions over variables
- **4.** Arithmetic equations that discard constraints over the given variables

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: 1. Arithmetic equations and inequalities that bind the values of variables**

## Solution

A constraint-satisfaction problem contains variables, a domain of possible values for each variable, and constraints that restrict which value combinations are permitted. Numerical constraints can be written as equations or inequalities; solving the CSP assigns values that satisfy all of them. Option 1 is the most complete description among those given.

## Key Points

- CSP = variables + domains + relations that restrict the allowed assignments.

## Why the other options are incorrect

Option 2 contradicts the purpose of a constraint: a condition that imposes no restriction does no useful constraining. Options 3 and 4 mention equations only and omit inequalities; option 4 also incorrectly says constraints are discarded rather than enforced.
