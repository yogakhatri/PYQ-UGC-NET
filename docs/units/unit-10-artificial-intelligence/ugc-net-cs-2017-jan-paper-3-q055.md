# Question 55

*UGC NET CS · 2017 Jan Paper 3 · Knowledge Representation and Reasoning · Modus Ponens and Modus Tollens*

Consider two named inference rules. R1: from α⊃β and α, infer β; this is called Modus Tollens. R2: from α⊃β and ¬β, infer ¬α; this is called Modus Ponens. Which naming statement is correct?

- **1.** Only R1 is correct.
- **2.** Only R2 is correct.
- **3.** Both R1 and R2 are correct.
- **4.** Neither R1 nor R2 is correct.

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: 4. Neither R1 nor R2 is correct.**

## Solution

The inference from α→β and α to β is valid, but its name is Modus Ponens, not Modus Tollens; therefore R1 is incorrectly named. The inference from α→β and ¬β to ¬α is also valid, but its name is Modus Tollens, not Modus Ponens; therefore R2 is incorrectly named. Neither naming statement is correct, so option 4 is the answer.

## Key Points

- Modus Ponens affirms the antecedent; Modus Tollens denies the consequent.

## Why the other options are incorrect

Options 1–3 accept at least one swapped label. The logical inferences themselves are sound—the defect is that the question's R1 and R2 attach the two standard names in reverse.
