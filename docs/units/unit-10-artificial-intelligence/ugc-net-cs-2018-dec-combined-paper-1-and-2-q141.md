# Question 141

*UGC NET CS · 2018 Dec Paper 1 And 2 · Intelligent Agents · Learning Agents and Performance Improvement*

An agent can improve its performance by

- **1.** Perceiving
- **2.** Responding
- **3.** Learning
- **4.** Observing

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: 3. Learning**

## Solution

A rational agent improves when experience changes its future behaviour in a way that raises the expected value of its performance measure. That enduring improvement is learning. Perception and observation supply information, and responding produces an action, but neither by itself guarantees that later decisions become better. Therefore learning is the performance-improving capability, option 3.

## Key Points

- In a learning agent, experience is converted into a lasting improvement in action selection or prediction.

## Why the other options are incorrect

Perceiving and observing acquire information about the environment; an agent can repeatedly sense the same facts without adapting. Responding is necessary for acting, but a fixed reflex agent responds without improving. Learning is the process that uses experience or feedback to modify the agent's knowledge or policy.
