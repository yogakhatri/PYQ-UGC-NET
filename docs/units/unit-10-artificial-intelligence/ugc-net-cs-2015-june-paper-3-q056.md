# Question 56

*UGC NET CS · 2015 June Paper 3 · Knowledge Representation · Frames, scripts and semantic networks*

Match the following knowledge representation techniques with their applications : List - I List - II (a) Frames (i) Pictorial representation of objects, their attributes and relationships (b) Conceptual dependencies (ii) To describe real world stereotype events (c) Associative networks (iii) Record like structures for grouping closely related knowledge (d) Scripts (iv) Structures and primitives to represent sentences Codes : (a) (b) (c) (d)

- **1.** (iii) (iv) (i) (ii)
- **2.** (iii) (iv) (ii) (i)
- **3.** (iv) (iii) (i) (ii)
- **4.** (iv) (iii) (ii) (i)

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: 1. (iii) (iv) (i) (ii)**

## Solution

Frames are record-like slot structures for related knowledge: (a)–(iii). Conceptual dependency uses conceptual primitives and dependency structures to represent sentence meaning: (b)–(iv). Associative or semantic networks depict objects, attributes, and relations as nodes and links: (c)–(i). Scripts describe stereotyped event sequences such as a restaurant visit: (d)–(ii). The sequence is `(iii),(iv),(i),(ii)`.

## Key Points

- Frames group slots, semantic networks link concepts, conceptual dependency represents sentence meaning, and scripts encode routine events.

## Why the other options are incorrect

Options 2–4 swap networks, scripts, frames, or conceptual dependency. The visual node-link nature of networks and event-sequence nature of scripts are decisive anchors for option 1.
