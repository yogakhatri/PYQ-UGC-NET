# Question 17

*UGC NET CS · 2010 June Paper 2 · Unsolvable Problems and Computational Complexity · Complexity Measurement and Classification*

Generalization is which kind of process?

- **A.** top-down
- **B.** bottom-up
- **C.** both top-down and bottom-up
- **D.** none of these

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: B. bottom-up**

## Solution

Generalization combines multiple lower-level entity types that share features into a higher-level supertype, so it proceeds bottom-up—for example, Car and Truck generalized as Vehicle.

## Key Points

- Generalization combines specifics upward; specialization divides a general type downward.

## Why the other options are incorrect

Top-down splitting of a broad type into narrower subtypes is specialization, the inverse process.
