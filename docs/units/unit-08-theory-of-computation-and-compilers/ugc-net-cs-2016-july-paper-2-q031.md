# Question 31

*UGC NET CS · 2016 July Paper 2 · Regular Language Models · Counting Fixed-Length Strings from Regular Expressions*

How many length-4 strings are generated by `(0|ε)1+2*(3|ε)`? Here `|` denotes alternation, `+` means one or more occurrences, `*` means zero or more occurrences, and ε is the empty string.

- **1.** 08
- **2.** 10
- **3.** 11
- **4.** 12

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: 4. 12**

## Solution

Every generated string has the form 0ᵃ1ᵇ2ᶜ3ᵈ, where a,d∈{0,1}, b≥1, c≥0, and length requires a+b+c+d=4. Count by s=a+d. For s=0 there are 4 choices for (b,c): (1,3) through (4,0). For s=1 there are 2 choices for which optional symbol appears and 3 choices for (b,c), giving 6. For s=2 there are 2 choices for (b,c). Total =4+6+2=12, so option 4 is correct.

## Key Points

- Translate a regular expression into repetition counts; optional symbols contribute 0 or 1, `+` contributes at least 1, and `*` contributes at least 0.

## Why the other options are incorrect

The counts 8, 10, and 11 miss valid cases involving one or both optional end symbols. A reliable method is to convert every quantifier into an integer constraint and count all nonnegative solutions instead of trying to list strings informally.
