# Question 17

*UGC NET CS · 2009 Dec Paper 2 · Unsolvable Problems and Computational Complexity · Complexity Measurement and Classification*

Specialization is which kind of process?

- **A.** top-down
- **B.** bottom-up
- **C.** both top-down and bottom-up
- **D.** none of these

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: A. top-down**

## Solution

Specialization begins with a broad supertype and defines narrower subtypes based on distinguishing properties, so it proceeds top-down. For example, Employee may be specialized into Faculty and Staff.

## Key Points

- Specialization splits a general type; generalization combines specific types.

## Why the other options are incorrect

Bottom-up combination of similar entity types into a supertype is generalization, not specialization.
