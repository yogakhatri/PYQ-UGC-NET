# Question 34

*UGC NET CS · 2018 July Paper 2 · Pushdown Automata · Recognition of Context-Free and Regular Languages*

A pushdown automaton can recognize languages generated by:

- **1.** Only context free grammar
- **2.** Only regular grammar
- **3.** Context free grammar or regular grammar
- **4.** Only context sensitive grammar

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: 3. Context free grammar or regular grammar**

## Solution

A nondeterministic pushdown automaton recognizes exactly the context-free languages generated by context-free grammars. Every regular language is also context-free, and a PDA can simply ignore its stack to simulate a finite automaton. Thus it can recognize languages generated by either a context-free grammar or a regular grammar, making option 3 the intended answer.

## Key Points

- Regular ⊂ context-free, and PDA power corresponds to the entire context-free family.

## Why the other options are incorrect

Option 2 is too narrow because PDAs recognize nonregular CFLs such as {aⁿbⁿ}. Option 4 is too strong because arbitrary context-sensitive languages require an LBA. Option 1 uses CFL as an umbrella class, but the question explicitly asks which listed grammar types are covered and provides the inclusive choice 3.
