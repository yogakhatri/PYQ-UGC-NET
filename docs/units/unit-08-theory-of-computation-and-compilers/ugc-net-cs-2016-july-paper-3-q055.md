# Question 55

*UGC NET CS · 2016 July Paper 3 · Regular Languages and Finite Automata · DFA-Induced Equivalence Relation*

Let L be the language generated by regular expression 0*10* and accepted by the deterministic finite automaton M. Consider the relation R_M defined by M. As all states are reachable from the start state, R_M has _____ equivalence classes.

- **1.** 2
- **2.** 4
- **3.** 5
- **4.** 6

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: No uniquely valid listed option — the DFA M is not specified; a minimal complete DFA would give 3 classes**

## Solution

For a fixed DFA M, define x R_M y when both strings take the start state to the same state. Every reachable state then corresponds to exactly one R_M equivalence class. The language 0*10* consists of binary strings containing exactly one 1, and its minimal complete DFA has three reachable states: no 1 seen, exactly one 1 seen, and at least two 1s (the dead state). It would therefore induce 3 classes, but 3 is absent from the options. More importantly, the question never specifies that M is minimal or gives its state set. A nonminimal DFA for the same language can have additional reachable states and hence a different number of R_M classes. Reachability alone does not determine the answer.

## Key Points

- R_M groups strings by the state reached in one particular DFA; R_L (Myhill-Nerode) groups strings by future language behavior and determines the minimal DFA.

## Why the other options are incorrect

The listed counts 2, 4, 5, and 6 can only be justified by silently choosing a particular partial or nonminimal automaton. A complete DFA needs the rejecting dead state, so the canonical minimal construction has 3 classes. Since other reachable, equivalent states may be added in a nonminimal M, none of the four numbers follows from the printed information.

## Additional Information

This is a defective item as printed. For exam revision, remember both facts: index(R_M) equals the number of reachable states of M, whereas index(R_L) equals the number of states in the minimal DFA.
