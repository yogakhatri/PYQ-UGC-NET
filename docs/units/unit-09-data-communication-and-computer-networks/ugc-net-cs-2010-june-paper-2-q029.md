# Question 29

*UGC NET CS · 2010 June Paper 2 · OSI and TCP/IP Layer Functions · Addressing*

What services does the Internet layer provide? 1. Quality of service; 2. Routing; 3. Addressing; 4. Connection-oriented delivery; 5. Framing bits.

- **A.** 1,2,3
- **B.** 2,3,4
- **C.** 1,3,4,5
- **D.** 2,3,4,5

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: A. 1,2,3**

## Solution

The Internet layer supplies logical addressing and routing, and its IP header includes service/traffic-class mechanisms used to request differentiated quality of service. It does not guarantee connection-oriented delivery, and framing belongs to the link/network-access layer. Therefore 1,2,3.

## Key Points

- IP provides addressing and routing with best-effort service; frames belong below it.

## Why the other options are incorrect

Options B–D include connection-oriented delivery, framing, or both, which are not Internet-layer IP services.
