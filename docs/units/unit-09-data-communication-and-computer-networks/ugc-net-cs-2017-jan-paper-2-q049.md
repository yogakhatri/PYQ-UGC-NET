# Question 49

*UGC NET CS · 2017 Jan Paper 2 · Mobile Technology · W-CDMA and UMTS Channel Bandwidth*

In 3G network, W-CDMA is also known as UMTS. The minimum spectrum allocation required for W-CDMA is _______.

- **1.** 2 MHz
- **2.** 20 KHz
- **3.** 5 KHz
- **4.** 5 MHz

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: 4. 5 MHz**

## Solution

The conventional 3.84 Mcps W-CDMA/UMTS carrier spreads information across approximately a 5 MHz radio channel. Among the listed allocations, 5 MHz is therefore the minimum single-carrier spectrum block intended by the question, so option 4 is correct.

## Key Points

- Classic UMTS/W-CDMA: 3.84 Mcps chip rate and approximately 5 MHz carrier bandwidth.

## Why the other options are incorrect

2 MHz is too narrow for the conventional wideband carrier, while 20 kHz and 5 kHz are narrowband channel spacings many orders smaller than a W-CDMA carrier.
