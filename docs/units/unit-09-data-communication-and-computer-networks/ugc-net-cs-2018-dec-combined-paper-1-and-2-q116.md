# Question 116

*UGC NET CS · 2018 Dec Paper 1 And 2 · Network Security · Firewalls and Virtual Private Networks*

Which statements are true? (i) Firewalls can screen traffic entering or leaving an organization. (ii) Virtual private networks can simulate an old leased network to provide desirable properties.

- **1.** (i) only
- **2.** (ii) only
- **3.** Both (i) and (ii)
- **4.** Neither (i) nor (ii)

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: 3. Both (i) and (ii)**

## Solution

Statement (i) is true: a firewall applies a security policy to network traffic crossing an organizational boundary, so it may inspect and permit or block both inbound and outbound traffic. Statement (ii) is also true: a VPN builds authenticated and usually encrypted tunnels over a shared network, giving separated sites or users properties similar to a private leased network without requiring a dedicated physical circuit. Both statements are true, option 3.

## Key Points

- Firewall = policy enforcement at a traffic boundary; VPN = private logical tunnel over shared infrastructure.

## Why the other options are incorrect

Options 1 and 2 omit one true statement. Option 4 rejects both established network-security mechanisms. A firewall is not limited to incoming traffic, and a VPN's purpose is precisely to emulate private connectivity over public/shared infrastructure.
