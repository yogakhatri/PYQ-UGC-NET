# Question 84

*UGC NET CS · 2020 Nov With Answers · Data Communication · Period–Frequency Conversion*

The period of a signal is 100 ms. Then the frequency of this signal in kilohertz is

- **1.** 10
- **2.** 10⁻¹
- **3.** 10⁻²
- **4.** 10⁻³

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: 3. 10⁻²**

## Solution

Frequency is the reciprocal of period: f=1/T. Convert 100 ms to seconds: T=100×10⁻³=0.1 s. Hence f=1/0.1=10 Hz. Because 1 kHz=1000 Hz, 10 Hz=10/1000=0.01 kHz=10⁻² kHz. Therefore option 3.

## Key Points

- Use f=1/T, convert milliseconds to seconds first, and convert hertz to kilohertz last.

## Why the other options are incorrect

10 kHz would mean 10,000 Hz and a period of 0.1 ms. The values 10⁻¹ and 10⁻³ kHz correspond to 100 Hz and 1 Hz respectively, whose periods are 10 ms and 1 s.
