# Question 29

*UGC NET CS · 2014 Dec Paper 3 · Data-Link Control · HDLC Bit-Oriented Protocol*

_____ is a bit-oriented protocol for communication over point-to-point and multipoint links.

- **A.** Stop-and-wait
- **B.** HDLC
- **C.** Sliding window
- **D.** Go-back-N

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: B. HDLC**

## Solution

HDLC (High-Level Data Link Control) is a bit-oriented data-link protocol. It defines framed communication and can operate on point-to-point as well as multipoint links. Its bit-oriented framing distinguishes it from character-oriented protocols.

## Key Points

- HDLC is the named bit-oriented protocol; the other options describe control mechanisms that protocols may employ.

## Why the other options are incorrect

Stop-and-wait, sliding window, and Go-Back-N are flow/error-control techniques or ARQ schemes, not the name of the requested general bit-oriented link protocol. HDLC may use window-based operation, but that does not make 'sliding window' a protocol name here.
