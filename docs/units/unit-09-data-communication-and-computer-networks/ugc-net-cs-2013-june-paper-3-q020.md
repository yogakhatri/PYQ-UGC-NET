# Question 20

*UGC NET CS · 2013 June Paper 3 · Data Communication · Decibel Power Ratios*

The ________ measures the relative strengths of two signals or a signal at two different points.

- **A.** frequency
- **B.** attenuation
- **C.** throughput
- **D.** decibel

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: D. decibel**

## Solution

A decibel expresses a logarithmic ratio between two power levels: dB=10 log10(P2/P1). For voltage or current across the same impedance the corresponding form is 20 log10(V2/V1). It therefore measures the relative strength of two signals or the same signal at two locations.

## Key Points

- Decibel is a logarithmic ratio, not an absolute signal-strength unit.

## Why the other options are incorrect

Frequency measures cycles per second. Attenuation is the loss itself and may be reported in decibels, but it is not the unit/measure named by the stem. Throughput is the achieved data-transfer rate.
