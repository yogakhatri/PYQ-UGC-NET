# Question 43

*UGC NET CS · 2013 Dec Paper 2 · Wide-Area Network Technologies · ATM for Long-Haul Traffic*

Which of the following is widely used inside the telephone system for long-haul data traffic ?

- **A.** ISDN
- **B.** ATM
- **C.** Frame Relay
- **D.** ISTN

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: B. ATM**

## Solution

ATM, or Asynchronous Transfer Mode, was designed for high-speed integrated voice, video and data transport using fixed 53-byte cells. Telephone carriers deployed it in backbone and long-haul networks because fixed-size cells support predictable switching and quality-of-service classes.

## Key Points

- Historical telecom association: ATM used fixed-size cells for carrier backbones and long-haul mixed traffic.

## Why the other options are incorrect

ISDN provides digital subscriber access rather than the principal long-haul backbone technology described. Frame Relay is a variable-length data service without ATM's cell-based integrated traffic design. ISTN is not the relevant standard.
