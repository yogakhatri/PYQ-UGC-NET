# Question 30

*UGC NET CS · 2016 July Paper 2 · Physical Layer · Optical Fiber and Total Internal Reflection*

Optical fiber uses reflection to guide light through a channel, in which angle of incidence is ________ the critical angle.

- **1.** equal to
- **2.** less than
- **3.** greater than
- **4.** less than or equal to

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: 3. greater than**

## Solution

An optical fiber confines light by total internal reflection at the higher-index core to lower-index cladding boundary. Total internal reflection occurs only when light travels from the denser medium and its incidence angle, measured from the normal, is greater than the critical angle. Therefore option 3 is correct.

## Key Points

- Total internal reflection requires denser-to-rarer travel and incidence angle θᵢ>θc.

## Why the other options are incorrect

At the critical angle, the refracted ray travels along the boundary; below it, part of the light refracts into the cladding. Neither equality nor a smaller angle gives the repeated total internal reflections needed for guidance.
