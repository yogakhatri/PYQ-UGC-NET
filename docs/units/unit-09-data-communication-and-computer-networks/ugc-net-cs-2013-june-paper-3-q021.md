# Question 21

*UGC NET CS · 2013 June Paper 3 · Transmission Media · Multidrop Thick Coaxial Cable*

Which one of the following media is multidrop ?

- **A.** Shielded Twisted pair cable
- **B.** Unshielded Twisted pair cable
- **C.** Thick Coaxial cable
- **D.** Fiber Optic cable

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: C. Thick Coaxial cable**

## Solution

Thick coaxial cable was used by 10BASE5 Ethernet as a shared bus. Multiple stations attached to the same cable segment through transceiver taps, making the medium multidrop. Every attached station observed transmissions on the common coaxial segment.

## Key Points

- Multidrop means several devices share and tap one physical medium; classic thick-coax Ethernet is the model example.

## Why the other options are incorrect

Twisted-pair Ethernet links are normally point-to-point between a station and a hub/switch, whether shielded or unshielded. Fiber links are likewise ordinarily point-to-point. Thick coax is the listed medium historically designed for taps along one shared bus.
