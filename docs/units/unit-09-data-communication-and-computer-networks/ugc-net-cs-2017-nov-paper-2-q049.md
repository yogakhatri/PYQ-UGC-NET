# Question 49

*UGC NET CS · 2017 Nov Paper 2 · Cloud Computing and IoT · Low-Power Wireless Technologies*

Which of the given wireless technologies used in IoT, consumes the least amount of power ?

- **1.** Zigbee
- **2.** Bluetooth
- **3.** Wi-Fi
- **4.** GSM/CDMA

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: 1. Zigbee**

## Solution

In the conventional IoT comparison intended by the 2017 question, Zigbee is designed for low-data-rate, short-range sensor and control networks where nodes may sleep for long periods and run on small batteries. It therefore consumes less power than classic Bluetooth, Wi-Fi, and cellular GSM/CDMA, so option 1 is correct.

## Key Points

- Wireless design trades range and throughput against energy; Zigbee/IEEE 802.15.4 targets low-power, low-rate IoT sensing and control.

## Why the other options are incorrect

Wi-Fi favors higher throughput at substantially greater energy cost, and cellular radios need long-range infrastructure communication. Classic Bluetooth is lower power than Wi-Fi/cellular but was not the lowest-power choice in this textbook comparison. Modern Bluetooth Low Energy changes product-specific comparisons, so interpret the option as the older generic Bluetooth category used by the exam.
