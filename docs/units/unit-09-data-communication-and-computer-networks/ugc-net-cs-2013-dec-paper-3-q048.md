# Question 48

*UGC NET CS · 2013 Dec Paper 3 · Data Communication · Transmission Impairments and Attenuation*

_____ is a transmission impairment in which a signal loses strength because of resistance in the transmission medium.

- **A.** Attenuation
- **B.** Distortion
- **C.** Noise
- **D.** Decibel

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: A. Attenuation**

## Solution

Attenuation is the gradual loss of signal amplitude or power as a signal propagates through a medium. Resistance converts some electrical energy to heat, so a received signal is weaker than the transmitted signal. Amplifiers or repeaters may be used to compensate over long distances.

## Key Points

- Attenuation means loss of strength; distortion means change of shape; noise means an unwanted added signal.

## Why the other options are incorrect

Distortion changes the signal's shape because components experience different delays or attenuation. Noise is unwanted added energy or interference. A decibel is a logarithmic unit used to express gain or loss; it is not itself a transmission impairment.
