# Question 39

*UGC NET CS · 2012 June Paper 2 · Mobile Technology · Bluetooth Time-Division Duplex*

For the transmission of the signal, Bluetooth wireless technology uses

- **A.** time division multiplexing
- **B.** frequency division multiplexing
- **C.** time division duplex
- **D.** frequency division duplex

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: C. time division duplex**

## Solution

Classic Bluetooth divides communication into time slots and alternates transmission direction between the master/central and slave/peripheral on the same frequency-hopping channel. Using different time slots for the two directions is time-division duplex (TDD). Frequency hopping changes channel over time but does not make the link frequency-division duplex.

## Key Points

- Bluetooth combines frequency hopping with time-slotted time-division duplex.

## Why the other options are incorrect

TDM describes sharing capacity among streams but not specifically the two directions. FDM and FDD use simultaneous distinct frequency bands, unlike Bluetooth's alternating slot directions.
