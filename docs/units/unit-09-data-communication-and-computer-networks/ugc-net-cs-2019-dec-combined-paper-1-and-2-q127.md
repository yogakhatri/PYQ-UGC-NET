# Question 127

*UGC NET CS · 2019 Dec Paper 1 And 2 · Transmission Media · Optical-Fiber Characteristics*

Consider the following statements: (a) Fiber optic cable is much lighter than copper cable. (b) Fiber optic cable is not affected by power surges or electromagnetic interference. (c) Optical transmission is inherently bidirectional. Which of the statements is (are) correct?

- **1.** Only (a) and (b)
- **2.** Only (a) and (c)
- **3.** Only (b) and (c)
- **4.** (a), (b) and (c)

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: 1. Only (a) and (b)**

## Solution

Statements (a) and (b) are correct. Optical fibre is made from thin glass or plastic strands and is substantially lighter than comparable copper cabling. Its signal is light confined within the fibre rather than an electrical current in a conductor, so electromagnetic interference and ordinary electrical power surges do not corrupt the transmitted signal. Statement (c) is false: a basic optical link is directional—a transmitter launches light and a receiver detects it. Full-duplex or bidirectional communication must be engineered using two fibres, opposite-direction wavelengths, circulators, or other multiplexing arrangements; it is not an inherent property of a single optical transmission path. Thus option 1.

## Key Points

- Fibre is light, electrically nonconductive, and immune to EMI; bidirectionality requires link design rather than arising automatically.

## Why the other options are incorrect

Options 2, 3, and 4 all accept statement (c). They confuse a bidirectional fibre communication system with the direction of one transmitter-to-receiver optical channel.
