# Question 19

*UGC NET CS · 2012 June Paper 2 · Data Communication · Fast Ethernet Physical Variants*

Which Fast Ethernet physical-layer variant supports a station-to-hub/link distance of about 2000 metres?

- **A.** 100BASE-TX
- **B.** 100BASE-FX
- **C.** 100BASE-T4
- **D.** 100BASE-T1

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: B. 100BASE-FX**

## Solution

100BASE-FX carries Fast Ethernet over optical fibre. In the commonly quoted full-duplex point-to-point configuration, multimode fibre supports a link of about 2 km. The copper twisted-pair variants are normally limited to roughly 100 m, so the 2000 m figure identifies 100BASE-FX.

## Key Points

- The F in 100BASE-FX signals fibre, enabling much longer Fast Ethernet links than twisted-pair T variants.

## Why the other options are incorrect

100BASE-TX and 100BASE-T4 are copper variants with much shorter segment limits. 100BASE-T1 is a later single-pair copper technology and is not the 2 km legacy Fast Ethernet option.
