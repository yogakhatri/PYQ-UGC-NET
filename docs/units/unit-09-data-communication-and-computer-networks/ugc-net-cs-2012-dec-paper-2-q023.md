# Question 23

*UGC NET CS · 2012 Dec Paper 2 · Transmission Media · RG-59 Coaxial Cable*

Coaxial cables categorized by Radio Government ratings are adapted for specialized functions. Category RG-59, with impedance 75 ohms, is used for:

- **1.** Cable TV
- **2.** Ethernet
- **3.** Thin Ethernet
- **4.** Thick Ethernet

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: 1. Cable TV**

## Solution

RG-59 is a 75-ohm coaxial cable traditionally used for baseband video and cable-television distribution. Its characteristic impedance matches common video/RF television systems.

## Key Points

- Cable association: RG-59 = 75 ohms = video/cable TV; legacy coaxial Ethernet uses 50-ohm cable.

## Why the other options are incorrect

Classic thick Ethernet used 50-ohm RG-8-type coax, and thin Ethernet used 50-ohm RG-58. 'Ethernet' alone does not identify the 75-ohm RG-59 application asked here.
