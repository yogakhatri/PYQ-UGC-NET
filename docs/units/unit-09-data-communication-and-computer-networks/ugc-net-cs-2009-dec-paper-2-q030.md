# Question 30

*UGC NET CS · 2009 Dec Paper 2 · OSI and TCP/IP Layer Functions · Token Passing*

Match the following : (i) Ethernet (a) Deterministic (ii) Token Ring (b) Utilize the full wire speed (iii) Cut-through switch (c) Prevent looping (iv) Spanning tree (d) Checking valid address Codes :

- **A.** i – d, ii – a, iii – b, iv – c
- **B.** i – a, ii – d, iii – b, iv – c
- **C.** i – d, ii – d, iii – c, iv – b
- **D.** i – d, i i – c, iii – b, iv – a

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: A. i – d, ii – a, iii – b, iv – c**

## Solution

Token Ring access is deterministic (ii-a). A cut-through switch begins forwarding before receiving the whole frame and can exploit full wire speed/low latency (iii-b). Spanning Tree prevents switching loops (iv-c). The remaining pairing is Ethernet with valid-address-code checking as worded (i-d). Therefore i-d, ii-a, iii-b, iv-c.

## Key Points

- Token passing is deterministic; cut-through forwards early; spanning tree removes loops.

## Why the other options are incorrect

The alternatives assign determinism to Ethernet or exchange the established cut-through and spanning-tree functions.
