# Question 26

*UGC NET CS · 2016 July Paper 3 · Multiplexing · FDM, WDM and TDM Classification*

Consider: (A) FDM can be applied when link bandwidth exceeds the combined bandwidth of the signals. (B) WDM is an analog multiplexing technique for combining optical signals. (C) WDM is a digital multiplexing technique. (D) TDM is a digital multiplexing technique. Which option is correct?

- **1.** A, B, C and D are true.
- **2.** A, B, C and D are false.
- **3.** A, B and D are false; C is true.
- **4.** A, B and D are true; C is false.

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: 4. A, B and D are true; C is false.**

## Solution

A is true because FDM separates simultaneous signals into nonoverlapping frequency bands and the link must accommodate their combined bands plus guards. B is true: WDM combines optical carriers of different wavelengths and is classified as analog multiplexing. C is therefore false. D is true because TDM interleaves digital time slots. Hence A, B and D are true while C is false.

## Key Points

- FDM and WDM separate carriers by frequency/wavelength; TDM separates digital streams by time slots.

## Why the other options are incorrect

Option 1 cannot be correct because B and C give contradictory classifications of WDM. Options 2 and 3 incorrectly reject established properties of FDM, WDM, or TDM.
