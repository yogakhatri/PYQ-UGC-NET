# Question 4

*UGC NET CS · 2013 Dec Paper 2 · Transmission Media · VLF and LF Ground-Wave Propagation*

The VLF and LF bands use which type of propagation for communication?

- **A.** Ground
- **B.** Sky
- **C.** Line of sight
- **D.** Space

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: A. Ground**

## Solution

Very-low-frequency and low-frequency radio waves have long wavelengths and can follow the curvature of Earth as ground waves. This propagation supports communication beyond the optical horizon and is used for applications such as navigation and long-range low-rate links.

## Key Points

- Frequency-propagation cue: VLF/LF use ground waves; HF commonly uses sky waves; VHF and above mainly use line of sight.

## Why the other options are incorrect

Sky-wave propagation is mainly associated with ionospheric reflection at higher HF frequencies. Line-of-sight and space-wave propagation dominate VHF and above, where waves do not follow Earth's surface as effectively.
