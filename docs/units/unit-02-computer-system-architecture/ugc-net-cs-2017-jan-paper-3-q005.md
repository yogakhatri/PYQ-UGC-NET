# Question 5

*UGC NET CS · 2017 Jan Paper 3 · Microprocessors · 8085 Power-Supply Requirement*

In 8085 microprocessor, the digit 5 indicates that the microprocessor needs

- **1.** –5 volts, +5 volts supply
- **2.** +5 volts supply only
- **3.** –5 volts supply only
- **4.** 5 MHz clock

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: 2. +5 volts supply only**

## Solution

The Intel 8085 was designed to operate from a single +5 V DC supply. The final digit distinguishes it from the older 8080 family design that required multiple supply voltages; it is not a clock-frequency label. Thus option 2 is correct.

## Key Points

- 8085: an 8-bit Intel microprocessor built for a single +5 V power supply.

## Why the other options are incorrect

A −5 V rail is not required, so options 1 and 3 fail. The 5 does not mean a 5 MHz clock; standard 8085 variants had clock specifications identified separately by part suffix and rating.
