# Question 47

*UGC NET CS · 2012 Dec Paper 2 · Digital Logic Circuits and Components · Logic-Family Characteristics*

Match: a. TTL; b. ECL; c. CMOS. 1. High fan-out; 2. Low propagation delay; 3. High power dissipation.

- **1.** a-3,b-2,c-1
- **2.** a-1,b-2,c-3
- **3.** a-1,b-3,c-2
- **4.** a-3,b-1,c-2

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: 1. a-3,b-2,c-1**

## Solution

For the one-to-one textbook comparison intended here, ECL is matched with low propagation delay because its transistors avoid saturation and it is the fastest of the three families. CMOS is matched with high fan-out because its inputs draw extremely small steady-state current. The remaining property, high power dissipation, is assigned to TTL relative to CMOS. Thus a-3, b-2, c-1.

## Key Points

- For this matching set: TTL-high power, ECL-low delay, CMOS-high fan-out.
- Use the forced one-to-one context when properties overlap in practice.

## Why the other options are incorrect

Options 2 and 3 assign high power dissipation to CMOS, contradicting its major low-static-power advantage. Option 4 fails to give ECL its characteristic low propagation delay. In broader comparisons ECL also consumes substantial power, but the forced one-to-one matching uses speed as ECL's distinguishing property.
