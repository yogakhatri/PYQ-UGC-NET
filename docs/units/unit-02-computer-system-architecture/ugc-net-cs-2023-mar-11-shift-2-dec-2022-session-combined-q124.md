## Solution

CDMA is a multiple-access mechanism for one shared channel (A–III). GSM combines FDMA and TDMA (B–IV). UMTS provides higher-rate mobile Internet (C–I). Wi-Fi connects devices over a wireless network (D–II).

## Key Points

- CDMA separates users by codes; GSM uses frequency/time slots; UMTS is 3G; Wi-Fi is WLAN.

## Why the other options are incorrect

Other mappings interchange cellular access methods, 3G service, and local wireless networking.
