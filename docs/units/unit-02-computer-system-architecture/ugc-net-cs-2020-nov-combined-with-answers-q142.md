# Question 142

*UGC NET CS · 2020 Nov With Answers · Memory Hierarchy · Magnetic-Disk Total Capacity*

What is the capacity of the disk, in bytes?

- **1.** 25,000 K
- **2.** 500,000 K
- **3.** 250,000 K
- **4.** 50,000 K

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: 2. 500,000 K**

## Solution

From Q141, each surface holds 50,000 KBytes. Five double-sided platters provide 5×2=10 recording surfaces. Thus disk capacity = 10×50,000 KBytes = 500,000 KBytes, giving option 2.

## Key Points

- Total disk capacity = bytes per surface × two surfaces per platter × number of platters.

## Why the other options are incorrect

Option 4 is the capacity of one surface, not the whole disk. Option 3 effectively counts only five surfaces and ignores that each platter is double-sided. Option 1 is only half of one surface's capacity.
