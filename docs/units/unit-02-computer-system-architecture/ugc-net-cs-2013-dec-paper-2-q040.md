# Question 40

*UGC NET CS · 2013 Dec Paper 2 · Digital Logic Circuits and Components · Half Adders, Full Adders and Inverters*

Which of the following statements are true ? I. A circuit that adds two bits, producing a sum bit and a carry bit is called half adder. II. A circuit that adds two bits, producing a sum bit and a carry bit is called full adder. III. A circuit that adds two bits and a carry bit producing a sum bit and a carry bit is called full adder. IV. A device that accepts the value of a Boolean variable as input and produces its complement is called an inverter.

- **A.** I & II
- **B.** II & III
- **C.** I, II, III
- **D.** I, III & IV

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: D. I, III & IV**

## Solution

I is true: a half adder adds two input bits and produces sum and carry. II is false because that description is a half adder, not a full adder. III is true: a full adder accepts two operand bits plus carry-in and produces sum and carry-out. IV is true because an inverter implements Boolean NOT.

## Key Points

- Half adder has two inputs; full adder has three inputs including carry-in; inverter outputs the complement.

## Why the other options are incorrect

A includes false statement II and omits true III and IV. B includes II and omits I and IV. C also includes II and omits IV. Only D contains exactly I, III and IV.
