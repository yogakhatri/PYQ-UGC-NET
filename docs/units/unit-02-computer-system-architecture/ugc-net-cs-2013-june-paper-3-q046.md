# Question 46

*UGC NET CS · 2013 June Paper 3 · Digital Logic Circuits and Components · Logic Families and Characteristics*

Match the following: a. TTL — i. High component density; b. ECL — ii. Low power consumption; c. MOS — iii. Evolution of diode-transistor logic; d. CMOS — iv. High-speed digital circuits. Codes: a b c d

- **A.** iii ii i iv
- **B.** i iv iii ii
- **C.** iii iv i ii
- **D.** i ii iii iv

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: C. iii iv i ii**

## Solution

TTL developed from diode-transistor logic, so a→iii. ECL avoids transistor saturation and is known for very high switching speed, so b→iv. MOS technology permits high component density, so c→i. CMOS draws very little static power because ideally only one transistor network conducts in a stable state, so d→ii. The resulting order is iii, iv, i, ii.

## Key Points

- Logic-family cues: TTL←DTL, ECL→speed, MOS→density, CMOS→low static power.

## Why the other options are incorrect

A assigns low power to ECL even though ECL is power-hungry. B assigns high density to TTL and the DTL lineage to MOS. D assigns low power to ECL and high speed to CMOS, missing the distinguishing textbook properties.
