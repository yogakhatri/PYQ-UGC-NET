# Question 33

*UGC NET CS · 2015 Dec Paper 3 · Computer Graphics · Graphics Primitives*

Which of the following graphic primitives are considered as the basic building blocks of computer graphics ? (a) Points (b) Lines (c) Polylines (d) Polygons Codes :

- **1.** (a) only
- **2.** (a) and (b)
- **3.** (a), (b) and (c)
- **4.** (a), (b), (c) and (d)

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: 4. (a), (b), (c) and (d)**

## Solution

Points are zero-dimensional primitives; lines connect points; polylines join sequences of line segments; and polygons form closed multi-edge regions. Graphics systems use all four as foundational geometric primitives from which more complex images and models are assembled. Thus (a)–(d) are all included.

## Key Points

- Common geometric primitives include points, line segments/polylines, and polygons.

## Why the other options are incorrect

Options 1–3 unnecessarily exclude one or more standard primitives. Polylines and polygons are compound in construction, but they are still exposed and treated as basic graphics primitives.
