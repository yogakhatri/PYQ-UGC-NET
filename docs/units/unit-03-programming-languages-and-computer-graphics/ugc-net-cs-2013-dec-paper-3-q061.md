# Question 61

*UGC NET CS · 2013 Dec Paper 3 · Computer-Graphics Input Devices · Trackball and Spaceball*

Which statement is not true about a trackball and/or spaceball? I. A trackball is a two-dimensional positioning device, whereas a spaceball provides six degrees of freedom. II. Unlike a trackball, a spaceball does not actually move. III. A trackball is a three-dimensional positioning device, whereas a spaceball provides six degrees of freedom.

- **A.** I & II
- **B.** II & III
- **C.** II only
- **D.** III only

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: D. III only**

## Solution

Statement I is true: an ordinary trackball controls two screen coordinates, while a spaceball senses three translational and three rotational components, giving six degrees of freedom. Statement II is also true in the intended device description: the trackball's ball is physically rotated, whereas a spaceball is essentially stationary and senses applied force/torque. Statement III is false because it calls the trackball a three-dimensional positioning device. Therefore only III is not true.

## Key Points

- Trackball: 2-D pointing by rolling a ball.
- Spaceball: fixed 3-D input controller sensing six degrees of freedom.

## Why the other options are incorrect

A incorrectly rejects both true statements I and II. B incorrectly rejects II along with III. C rejects only II, although II describes the stationary force-sensing nature of a spaceball. D isolates the sole false statement.
