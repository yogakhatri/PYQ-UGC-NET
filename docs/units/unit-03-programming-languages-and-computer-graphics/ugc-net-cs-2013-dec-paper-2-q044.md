# Question 44

*UGC NET CS · 2013 Dec Paper 2 · Web Programming and E-Commerce · ANSI X12 EDI Standards*

The document standards for EDI were first developed by large business house during the 1970s and are now under the control of the following standard organisation :

- **A.** ISO
- **B.** ANSI
- **C.** ITU-T
- **D.** IEEE

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: B. ANSI**

## Solution

In the United States, cross-industry EDI document standards are maintained through ANSI's Accredited Standards Committee X12. The X12 transaction sets standardize business documents such as purchase orders, invoices and shipping notices.

## Key Points

- EDI standards cue: ANSI ASC X12 is the major U.S.
- business-document standard; UN/EDIFACT is the international counterpart.

## Why the other options are incorrect

ISO publishes the international EDIFACT family through UN-related standardization work but is not the organization intended by this history of the early U.S. business standards. ITU-T focuses on telecommunications, and IEEE is best known for electrical, computing and networking standards.
