# Question 30

*UGC NET CS · 2013 June Paper 3 · Computer Graphics · Resolution in Line Pairs per Inch*

If 40 black lines interleaved with 40 white lines can be distinguished across one inch, the resolution is

- **A.** 40 line-pairs per inch
- **B.** 80 line-pairs per inch
- **C.** 1600 lines per inch
- **D.** 40 lines per inch

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: A. 40 line-pairs per inch**

## Solution

One resolvable line pair consists of one black line plus one adjacent white line. Forty black lines interleaved with forty white lines therefore form forty black-white pairs across one inch, giving a spatial resolution of 40 line-pairs per inch.

## Key Points

- Line-pair resolution counts black-white cycles, not individual lines.

## Why the other options are incorrect

80 counts individual lines but labels them incorrectly as 80 pairs. 1600 multiplies unrelated counts. 40 lines per inch counts only one color's lines and uses the wrong unit for the stated black-white resolving pattern.
