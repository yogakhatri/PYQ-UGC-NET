# Question 18

*UGC NET CS · 2014 Dec Paper 3 · 3-D Object Representation, Geometric Transformations and Viewing · Cavalier, Cabinet, Isometric and Orthographic Projections*

Match the projection types with their properties: a. Cavalier projection; b. Cabinet projection; c. Isometric projection; d. Orthographic projection. i. Lines perpendicular to the xy-plane are not foreshortened. ii. Such lines are foreshortened to half their length. iii. The projection direction makes equal angles with all principal axes. iv. The projection direction is perpendicular to the view plane. Choose the code in the order a, b, c, d.

- **A.** i iii iv ii
- **B.** ii iii i iv
- **C.** iv ii iii i
- **D.** i ii iii iv

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: D. i ii iii iv**

## Solution

In a cavalier projection, the receding axis keeps full scale, so there is no foreshortening: a→i. Cabinet projection uses half scale on the receding axis: b→ii. Isometric projection treats the three principal axes symmetrically, so its viewing direction makes equal angles with them: c→iii. Orthographic projection uses projectors perpendicular to the view plane: d→iv. The sequence is i, ii, iii, iv.

## Key Points

- Remember the scale cues: cavalier = full receding scale, cabinet = half receding scale, isometric = equal axes, orthographic = perpendicular projectors.

## Why the other options are incorrect

A assigns cabinet and orthographic properties incorrectly. B gives cavalier the half-scale property and disrupts the other matches. C wrongly assigns the perpendicular-to-view-plane definition to cavalier. D alone preserves all four defining properties.
