# Question 14

*UGC NET CS · 2014 Dec Paper 3 · Interactive Computer Graphics · Input-Device Categories*

Match the following: a. Tablet, joystick — i. Continuous devices; b. Light pen, touch screen — ii. Direct devices; c. Locator, keyboard — iii. Logical devices; d. Data glove, sonic pen — iv. 3D interaction devices. Codes: a b c d

- **A.** ii i iv iii
- **B.** i iv iii ii
- **C.** i ii iii iv
- **D.** iv iii ii i

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: C. i ii iii iv**

## Solution

Tablet and joystick report continuously varying position/direction, so a→i. Light pen and touch screen select directly on the display, b→ii. Locator and keyboard are logical input-device classes exposed by graphics systems, c→iii. Data glove and sonic pen support spatial/3D interaction, d→iv. The sequence is i, ii, iii, iv.

## Key Points

- Graphics input cues: tablet/joystick continuous, light pen/touch direct, locator/keyboard logical, glove/sonic pen 3D.

## Why the other options are incorrect

A, B and D permute device categories in ways that separate obvious direct devices from direct interaction or assign 3D devices to continuous/logical groups. C preserves every standard pairing.
