# Question 15

*UGC NET CS · 2017 Jan Paper 3 · Computer Graphics · LCD Construction and Polarization*

Match the following : a. Glass i. Contains liquid crystal and serves as a bonding surface for a conductive coating. b. Conductive coating ii. Acts as a conductor so that a voltage can be applied across the liquid crystal. c. Liquid crystal iii. A substance which will polarize light when a voltage is applied to it. d. Polarized film iv. A transparent sheet that polarizes light. Codes : a b c d

- **1.** i ii iii iv
- **2.** i iii ii iv
- **3.** iv iii ii i
- **4.** iv ii i iii

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: 1. i ii iii iv**

## Solution

Glass forms the transparent plates, contains the liquid crystal, and provides a bonding surface for the conductive layer, so a→i. The conductive coating acts as an electrode through which voltage is applied, so b→ii. The liquid-crystal material changes the polarization behavior of light under the electric field, so c→iii. Polarizing film is the transparent sheet that polarizes light, so d→iv. The sequence i, ii, iii, iv is option 1.

## Key Points

- LCD stack: glass substrate → transparent conductive electrode → liquid crystal → polarizing film, with each layer serving a distinct optical or electrical role.

## Why the other options are incorrect

The other codes interchange physical layers with incompatible jobs. Glass is the structural substrate, the conductive coating carries the applied voltage, the liquid crystal is the electro-optic medium, and the polarizer selects polarization.
