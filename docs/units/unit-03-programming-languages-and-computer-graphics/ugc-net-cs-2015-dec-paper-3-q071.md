# Question 71

*UGC NET CS · 2015 Dec Paper 3 · Display Devices · Cathode-Ray Tube Components*

Which listed items are components of a cathode-ray tube (CRT)? (a) Electron gun, (b) Control electrode, (c) Focusing electrode, (d) Phosphor-coated screen.

- **1.** (a) and (d)
- **2.** (a), (b) and (d)
- **3.** (a), (b), (c) and (d)
- **4.** (a), (c) and (d)

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: 3. (a), (b), (c) and (d)**

## Solution

A CRT needs an electron gun to emit the beam, a control electrode/grid to regulate beam intensity, focusing electrodes to narrow the beam into a sharp spot, and a phosphor-coated screen that emits visible light where struck. All four listed parts are components, so option 3 is correct.

## Key Points

- CRT path: generate → control → focus/deflect → excite phosphor screen.

## Why the other options are incorrect

Each other option omits at least one functional stage: beam-intensity control or beam focusing. A usable CRT image requires emission, modulation, focusing/acceleration and the luminescent screen (with deflection hardware also present though not listed).
