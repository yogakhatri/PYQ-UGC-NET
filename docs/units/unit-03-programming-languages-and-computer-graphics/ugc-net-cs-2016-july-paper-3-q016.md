# Question 16

*UGC NET CS · 2016 July Paper 3 · 3-D Object Representation, Geometric Transformations and Viewing · Perspective Projection and Topological Distortion*

In perspective projection, if a line segment joining a point which lies in front of the viewer to a point in back of the viewer is projected to a broken line of infinite extent. This is known as _______.

- **1.** View confusion
- **2.** Vanishing point
- **3.** Topological distortion
- **4.** Perspective foreshortening

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: 3. Topological distortion**

## Solution

A segment crossing the plane through the center of projection contains a point whose perspective denominator becomes zero. As points approach it from opposite sides, their projected positions run toward opposite infinities, so one connected 3-D segment becomes two unbounded image pieces. This loss of connectedness is called topological distortion.

## Key Points

- Perspective topological distortion occurs when projection across the center-of-projection plane breaks connectedness and sends points to infinity.

## Why the other options are incorrect

View confusion refers to objects behind the center of projection appearing inverted. A vanishing point is where projections of parallel lines appear to converge. Perspective foreshortening makes distant objects or dimensions appear smaller. None describes a connected segment becoming a broken infinite image.
