# Question 63

*UGC NET CS · 2013 Dec Paper 3 · Computer Animation · Principles of Animation*

Consider three basic rules: I. Squash and stretch. II. Slow-in and slow-out. III. Stage the action properly. These rules are applied in

- **A.** Rendering
- **B.** Morphing
- **C.** Animation
- **D.** All the above

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: C. Animation**

## Solution

Squash and stretch conveys weight and flexibility; slow-in/slow-out models acceleration and deceleration by changing frame spacing; staging presents an action so its meaning is unmistakable. These are classic principles for creating convincing animation, not algorithms that define rendering or image morphing.

## Key Points

- Squash-and-stretch, slow-in/slow-out and staging are animation principles that communicate motion and intent.

## Why the other options are incorrect

Rendering converts a scene description into pixels and may be used to produce animation frames, but these are not rendering rules. Morphing interpolates one shape or image into another. Since the three named rules specifically belong to animation, 'all the above' is too broad.
