# Question 28

*UGC NET CS · 2013 June Paper 3 · Display Devices · Critical Fusion Frequency*

The refresh rate above which a picture stops flickering and fuses into a steady image is called _________.

- **A.** Crucial fusion frequency
- **B.** Current frequency fusion
- **C.** Critical fusion frequency
- **D.** Critically diffused frequency

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: C. Critical fusion frequency**

## Solution

Critical fusion frequency (also called critical flicker-fusion frequency) is the threshold at which a periodically refreshed light or picture is perceived as continuous and steady rather than flickering. Displays must refresh above an appropriate threshold for comfortable viewing.

## Key Points

- Critical flicker fusion is the perceptual transition from separate flashes to a steady image.

## Why the other options are incorrect

Crucial fusion frequency is a plausible-sounding but nonstandard phrase. Current frequency fusion and critically diffused frequency are not the recognized visual-perception terms.
