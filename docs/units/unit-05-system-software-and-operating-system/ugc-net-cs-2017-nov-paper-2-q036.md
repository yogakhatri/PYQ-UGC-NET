# Question 36

*UGC NET CS · 2017 Nov Paper 2 · Distributed Systems · Scalability under Increasing Service Load*

In Distributed system, the capacity of a system to adapt the increased service load is called __________ .

- **1.** Tolerance
- **2.** Scalability
- **3.** Capability
- **4.** Loading

> [!TIP]
> **Correct answer: 2. Scalability**

## Solution

Scalability is a system's ability to continue providing acceptable service as workload, users, data volume, or geographic distribution grows—usually by adding resources or reorganizing work. The capacity to adapt to increased service load is therefore scalability, option 2.

## Key Points

- A scalable distributed system handles growth without a disproportionate loss of performance or manageability.

## Why the other options are incorrect

Tolerance normally refers to continuing despite faults. Capability is too general and is not the named distributed-systems property. Loading describes workload or the act of loading, not the ability to grow with it.
