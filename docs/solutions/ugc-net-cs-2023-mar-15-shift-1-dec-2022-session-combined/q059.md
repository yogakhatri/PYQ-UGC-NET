# Question 59

In the context of Binding which one of the following is incorrect?

- **1.** Static binding is done at Compile Time
- **2.** Absolute addresses are known at Load Time
- **3.** Internal functions are known at link time
- **4.** Target of a function is known at runtime

## Answer

**3. Internal functions are known at link time**

## Detailed solution

Static binding may be decided at compile time; relocation makes absolute addresses known at load time; dynamic dispatch may choose a target at runtime. Calls to internal functions are normally resolvable within the compilation unit rather than waiting for external symbol resolution at link time, making statement 3 the incorrect one.

## Why the other options do not work

Statements 1,2,4 correspond to recognized compile/load/runtime binding stages.

## Key idea to remember

Link time primarily resolves references between separately compiled modules.
