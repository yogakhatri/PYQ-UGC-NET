# Question 147

Read the passage carefully and answer the question that follows For a knapsack problem, P and W are profits and weights for a set of 7 items as given below: P= (9,5,2,7,6,16,9} W= (2,5,6,11,1,9,3} What is the maximum value of items you can carry if maximum weight allowed is 25 with a fractional knapsack?

- **1.** 48.1
- **2.** 49
- **3.** 47.9
- **4.** 40

## Answer

**1. 48.1**

## Detailed solution

For capacity 25, first take items 5,1,7,6: weight 15, profit 40. Take item 2 fully: weight becomes 20 and profit 45. The remaining capacity 5 takes 5/11 of item 4, adding (5/11)×7=35/11≈3.1818. Total profit is 48.1818, represented by option 48.1.

## Why the other options do not work

49 and 47.9 do not equal the ratio-greedy calculation, while 40 ignores the additional ten units of capacity.

## Key idea to remember

After all higher-ratio items, fill the remaining capacity with the required fraction of the next item.
