# Question 22

The number of edges in a complete graph of n vertices is

- **A.** n
- **B.** n(n – 1)/2
- **C.** n(n + 1)/2
- **D.** n 2/2

## Answer

**B. n(n – 1)/2**

## Detailed solution

A complete graph has one edge for every unordered pair of distinct vertices. The number of such pairs is C(n,2)=n(n−1)/2.

## Why the other options do not work

The other expressions do not count unordered pairs: n counts only linearly, n(n+1)/2 includes extra self-like terms, and n²/2 is not generally even an integer.

## Key idea to remember

Edges of Kₙ correspond one-to-one with 2-element vertex subsets.
