# Question 18

Match the following : (1) Determinants (a) No attribute can be added (2) Candidate key (b) Uniquely identified a row (3) Non-redundancy (c) A constraint between two attribute (4) Functional dependency (d) Group of attributes on the left hand s ide of arrow of function dependency.

- **A.** 1 – d, 2 – b, 3 – a, 4 – c
- **B.** 2 – d, 3 – a, 1 – b, 4 – c
- **C.** 4 – a, 3 – b, 2 – c, 1 – d
- **D.** 3 – a, 4 – b, 1 – c, 2 – d

## Answer

**A. 1 – d, 2 – b, 3 – a, 4 – c**

## Detailed solution

A determinant is the attribute group on the left side of a functional dependency (1-d). A candidate key uniquely identifies a row (2-b). The listed non-redundancy/minimality description pairs with no attribute being addable as stated (3-a). A functional dependency is a constraint between attribute sets (4-c). This yields 1-d,2-b,3-a,4-c.

## Why the other options do not work

The alternative mappings treat a key or dependency as the left-hand determinant and mismatch row identification with unrelated terms.

## Key idea to remember

In X→Y, X is the determinant; a candidate key uniquely identifies tuples.
