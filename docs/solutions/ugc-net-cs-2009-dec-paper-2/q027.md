# Question 27

What is the maximum window size in sliding window protocol used in a computer network ?

- **A.** 4
- **B.** 8
- **C.** 15
- **D.** 16

## Answer

**Cannot be determined from the question — window size depends on sequence-number bits and protocol variant**

## Detailed solution

There is no protocol-independent numeric maximum. With an m-bit sequence field, Go-Back-N normally permits at most 2^m−1 outstanding frames, while Selective Repeat normally limits the window to 2^(m−1) to avoid ambiguity. The stem supplies neither m nor the sliding-window variant, so 4, 8, 15, and 16 cannot be selected uniquely. For example, m=4 would give 15 for Go-Back-N but 8 for Selective Repeat.

## Why the other options do not work

Each listed number becomes plausible only after adding an unstated sequence-space size and protocol assumption.

## Key idea to remember

Window limits are derived from the finite sequence-number space and retransmission protocol.
