# Question 8

What is the transitive voltage for the voltage input of a CMOS operating from 10V supply ?

- **A.** 1V
- **B.** 2V
- **C.** 5V
- **D.** 10 V

## Answer

**C. 5V**

## Detailed solution

For a symmetric CMOS inverter, the switching/transition threshold is approximately halfway between the supply rails. With VDD=10 V and ground at 0 V, the transition voltage is about VDD/2=5 V.

## Why the other options do not work

1 V and 2 V are too close to the low rail for the nominal symmetric threshold; 10 V is the supply rail, not the transition point.

## Key idea to remember

A balanced CMOS inverter switches near half the supply voltage.
