# Question 34

Which of the following is the most general phase structured grammar ?

- **A.** Regular
- **B.** Context-sensitive
- **C.** Context free
- **D.** None of the above

## Answer

**B. Context-sensitive**

## Detailed solution

In the Chomsky hierarchy, regular grammars are contained in context-free grammars, which are contained in context-sensitive grammars. Among the listed phrase-structure classes, context-sensitive grammar is therefore the most general.

## Why the other options do not work

Regular and context-free grammars generate strictly narrower language classes. 'None' is false because context-sensitive is offered.

## Key idea to remember

Regular ⊂ context-free ⊂ context-sensitive ⊂ unrestricted.
