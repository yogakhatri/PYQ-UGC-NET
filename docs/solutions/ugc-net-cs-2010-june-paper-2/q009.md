# Question 9

What is decimal equivalent of BCD 11011.1100 ?

- **A.** 22.0
- **B.** 22.2
- **C.** 20.2
- **D.** 21.2

## Answer

**B. 22.2**

## Detailed solution

The printed bit string is not already valid digit-by-digit 8421 BCD: padded groups are 0001 1011 . 1100, and 1011/1100 exceed 9. Applying standard BCD correction (+0110 to each invalid tetrad) gives 0001 (1011+0110) . (1100+0110) = 0010 0010 . 0010 after carries. These are decimal digits 2,2.2, so the intended result is 22.2.

## Why the other options do not work

22.0, 20.2, and 21.2 do not follow the correction/carry process. Strictly speaking, the original groups are invalid as a canonical BCD numeral; the answer relies on the exercise's intended correction interpretation.

## Key idea to remember

A four-bit BCD group above 1001 is corrected by adding 0110 and propagating the carry.
