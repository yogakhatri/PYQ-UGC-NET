# Question 92

Consider the following statements: A. There exists a Boolean algebra with '5' elements. B. Every element of Boolean algebra has unique complement. If a Lattice 'L' is a Boolean algebra then L' is not relatively complemented. D. The direct product of two Boolean Algebras is also a Boolean algebra Choose the correct answer about the four statements given above.

- **1.** Only A and D are correct
- **2.** Only B and Dare correct
- **3.** All statements are NOT correct
- **4.** All statements are correct

## Answer

**2. Only B and Dare correct**

## Detailed solution

A finite Boolean algebra has 2^k elements, so five elements are impossible (A false). Complements are unique (B true). Boolean algebras are relatively complemented, so C is false. Direct products preserve the Boolean operations and axioms, so D is true. Only B and D are correct.

## Why the other options do not work

Every alternative other than option 2 either accepts the impossible five-element algebra or rejects one of the standard closure/complement properties.

## Key idea to remember

Finite Boolean algebra sizes are powers of two, and complements are unique.
