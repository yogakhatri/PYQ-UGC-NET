## Detailed solution

A deterministic k-tape Turing machine reads one tape symbol from each of its k tapes, so its input to Оґ is a state in Q and a k-tuple in О“^k. One transition selects the next state, writes one symbol on each tape, and chooses one movement L, R, or S for each head. Thus Оґ: QГ—О“^k в†’ QГ—О“^kГ—{L,R,S}^k.

## Why the other options do not work

The powerset 2^Q is associated with sets of states in nondeterministic constructions, not an ordinary deterministic transition. Adding a second Q or both Q and 2^Q introduces components that a multi-tape step does not consume or produce.

## Key idea to remember

For k tapes, both the symbols read/written and the head movements are k-tuples.
