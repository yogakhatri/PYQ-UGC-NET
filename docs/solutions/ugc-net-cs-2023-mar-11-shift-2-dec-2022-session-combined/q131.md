# Question 131

Given below are two statements: one is labeled as Assertion A and the other is labelled as Reason R. Assertion A: Cohesion is a qualitative indication of the degree to which a module can be written more compactly and is able to complete its function in a timely manner Reason R: Cohesion is a qualitative indication of the degree to which a module is connected to other modules and the outside world. In the light of the above statements, choose the most appropriate answer from the options given below.

- **1.** Both A and R are correct and R is the correct explanation of A.
- **2.** Both A and R are correct but R is NOT the correct explanation of A.
- **3.** A is correct but R is not correct.
- **4.** A is not correct but R is correct.

## Answer

**No listed option — both Assertion A and Reason R are incorrect**

## Detailed solution

Cohesion measures how strongly the responsibilities within one module belong together; it is not compactness or timely completion, so A is false. Connection to other modules/outside world describes coupling, not cohesion, so R is also false. No option states that both are false.

## Why the other options do not work

Options 1–2 call both true; option 3 calls A true; option 4 calls R true.

## Key idea to remember

High cohesion is internal unity; low coupling is weak inter-module dependence.
