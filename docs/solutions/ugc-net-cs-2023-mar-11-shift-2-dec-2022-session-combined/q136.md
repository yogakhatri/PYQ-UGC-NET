# Question 136

2BID: 187081 Four persons: P. Q. R and S are in police custody and one of them has committed a crime. They confess as follows: A. Person P: Q did it. B. Person Q: S did it. C. Person R: I did not do it. D. Person S: Q lied. If exactly one of the statements is false, which of the following is the guilty person.

- **1.** P
- **2.** Q
- **3.** R
- **4.** S

## Answer

**2. Q**

## Detailed solution

Assume Q is guilty. P's ‘Q did it’ is true; Q's ‘S did it’ is false; R's ‘I did not do it’ is true; and S's ‘Q lied’ is true because Q's statement is false. Exactly one statement is false, satisfying the condition.

## Why the other options do not work

Testing P, R, or S as guilty produces more than one false statement or otherwise violates the condition.

## Key idea to remember

For finite truth puzzles, test each candidate and count false statements explicitly.
